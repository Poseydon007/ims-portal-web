<h2 id="s1" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">1. Purpose</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;">To minimize risks associated with fatigue among personnel working on True East Mining exploration drilling sites in Saudi Arabia. This procedure ensures a safe, healthy, and productive work environment by proactively identifying fatigue hazards, implementing robust controls, and promoting a strong culture of awareness and non-punitive reporting. It aligns with ISO 45001:2018 and the KSA occupational health and safety framework.</p>

<h2 id="s2" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">2. Scope</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;">This procedure applies to all True East Mining employees, contractors, sub-contractors and visitors working at or visiting company drilling sites, camps and project locations. It is particularly critical for personnel involved in shift work, night operations, long travel, operation of heavy or rotating equipment, and work in high-heat desert conditions.</p>

<h2 id="s5" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">5. Fatigue Risk Factors and Indicators</h2>

<h3 class="text-xs font-bold mb-2 mt-4" style="color: #081C2E;">5.1 Primary Risk Factors</h3>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1 mb-4">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>High ambient temperatures and severe heat exposure (KSA desert climate, summer WBGT frequently &gt;30 °C).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Long travel durations to and from remote exploration sites.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Extended working hours and continuous shift patterns during drilling campaigns.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Night shifts and the resulting circadian rhythm disruption.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Cultural and religious practices — e.g., fasting and altered sleep schedules during Ramadan.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Poor sleep quality, dehydration, high physical workload or compounding personal and medical factors.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Camp conditions: noise, light, temperature control and sleeping arrangements.</span></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="text-xs font-bold mb-2 mt-4" style="color: #081C2E;">5.2 Physical and Behavioural Risk Indicators</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-2" style="color: #374151;">Supervisors, the buddy system and peers must actively monitor for the following signs:</p>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Reduced alertness, sluggish reaction times or lack of concentration.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Uncharacteristic irritability, mood changes or social withdrawal.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Increased frequency of operational mistakes, near misses or dropped tools.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Physical signs: constant yawning, micro-sleeps, heavy eyelids, poor coordination, frequent stumbling.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Slurred or slow speech, memory lapses, forgetting recent instructions.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Self-reported symptoms: headache, blurred vision, nausea, muscle weakness.</span></li>
</ul>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-4" style="color: #374151;">To prevent chronic fatigue, True East Mining enforces the following rostering baselines. Any deviation must be risk-assessed and authorised in writing by the Site Manager with HSE Manager endorsement.</p>

Any extension beyond 12 hours requires a written fatigue risk assessment.</td></tr>

      <tr style="background-color: rgba(8,28,46,0.03);"><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs font-medium" style="color: #081C2E; width: 35%;">Travel Buffer</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151;">Travel time to/from site is not counted as rest time. A minimum 1-hour buffer is required after arrival before starting work.</td></tr>
      <tr><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs font-medium" style="color: #081C2E; width: 35%;">Ramadan / Peak Heat Provisions</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151;">During Ramadan or summer heat waves (WBGT &gt;32 °C), rosters must include additional mandatory breaks and shorter overall shifts where operationally feasible. Iftar and Suhoor breaks are protected.</td></tr>
      <tr style="background-color: rgba(8,28,46,0.03);"><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs font-medium" style="color: #081C2E; width: 35%;">Emergency Call-Out</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151;">Any call-out during a rest period must be logged; the rest period must be re-started and the individual released from the next scheduled shift if the call-out exceeds 2 hours.</td></tr>

<h2 id="s7" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">7. Heat and Fatigue Controls</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #374151;">Heat stress directly accelerates physical fatigue in the KSA operating environment. The following controls are mandatory and integrated with the Heat Stress Prevention Procedure (TE-IMS-PROC-HSE-002).</p>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>WBGT Monitoring — adjust work-rest cycles dynamically based on the WBGT index — e.g., mandate 15 minutes of rest per hour when WBGT &gt;30 °C and 30 minutes per hour when WBGT &gt;32 °C.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Hydration — provide a minimum of 3–5 litres of cool drinking water per person per day, plus electrolyte replacements during heat events.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Cooling Stations — establish shaded rest areas and cooling stations equipped with fans or AC units (where available) near the active rig.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Scheduling — schedule the most physically demanding tasks (tripping rods, core handling, manual drilling tasks) outside of peak midday heat whenever possible.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Clothing — provide and enforce lightweight flame-resistant (FR) clothing, wide-brim or neck-flap hard hats, and sun protection.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Medical Surveillance — workers with known medical conditions that increase heat/fatigue risk are identified by pre-employment medical screening and managed by HR/HSE.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s8" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">8. Operational Control Measures</h2>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Buddy System — strict buddy system during critical or night operations — buddies actively watch for fatigue signs in partners and intervene without hesitation.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Pre-Shift Assessment — use fatigue assessment checklists or digital wellness tools (e.g., pre-shift wellness questionnaire) prior to clocking in. Outcomes recorded on the Fatigue Checklist Form.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Non-Punitive Reporting — actively encourage self-declaration of fatigue. There is zero penalty for a worker safely removing themselves from a high-risk task due to exhaustion. Stop Work Authority applies.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Targeted Monitoring — monitor high-risk tasks (rig console, driving, rod handling) with increased supervisory frequency during the fatigue window — typically 02:00–05:00 on night shifts.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Task Rotation — rotate repetitive or monotonous tasks between team members where feasible to reduce monotony-induced fatigue.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Driving Controls — no driving of light or heavy vehicles after more than 12 consecutive hours on shift. Refer to TE-IMS-SOP-LOG-001 Driving Safety SOP.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Alcohol, Drugs and Medications — zero-alcohol policy; employees must disclose use of prescription or over-the-counter medications that may cause drowsiness.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s9" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">9. Training and Awareness</h2>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Mandatory Training — all personnel complete fatigue management training during initial induction, followed by annual refresher. Drivers and equipment operators receive enhanced training.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Curriculum — recognising fatigue signs, personal risk factors, site controls, heat stress management, sleep hygiene, and the non-punitive reporting culture.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Seasonal Campaigns — targeted awareness campaigns immediately prior to Ramadan and the summer peak heat season (May–September).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Toolbox Talks — fatigue is a standing topic in the Toolbox Talk cycle during night shift rotations and during Ramadan.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Records — document all training and attendance records in the Training and Competence Matrix (TE-IMS-REG-TRN-001).</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s10" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">10. Incident Reporting and Review</h2>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Reporting — all fatigue-related incidents — including near misses caused by micro-sleep, heat exhaustion or loss of concentration — must be reported immediately using the Flash Notification Form (TE-IMS-FRM-HSE-002) and the Incident Investigation Form (TE-IMS-FRM-HSE-022).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Investigation — conduct root-cause investigation per the Continual Improvement and CAPA Procedure (TE-IMS-PROC-SYS-005).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Action — lessons learned must be used to adjust site rosters, shift lengths, cooling provisions or training content. Actions tracked in the CAR Log (TE-IMS-REG-SYS-004).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Trend Analysis — HSE Manager performs quarterly trend analysis on fatigue incidents and reports to Management Review.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s11" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">11. Monitoring, Audit and Continuous Improvement</h2>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1 mb-4">
