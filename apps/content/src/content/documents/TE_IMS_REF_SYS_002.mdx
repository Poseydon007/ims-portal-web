<h2 id="s1" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">1. Purpose</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #374151;">This document clearly defines how Finance &amp; Accounting documents and records are handled within True East Mining Company's Integrated Management System (IMS).</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-2" style="color: #374151;">The objective is to:</p>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1 mb-3">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span>Protect the confidentiality, integrity, and restricted access required for sensitive financial data.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span>Avoid unnecessary duplication or double-handling of records that are already generated and controlled by specialized accounting/financial software (ERP).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span>Maintain full IMS compliance and audit readiness while respecting the legal, regulatory, and operational realities of financial management.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span>Provide transparent, unambiguous rules so that all employees, auditors, and external parties understand the boundaries of the IMS.</span></li>
</ul>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;">This clarification prevents confusion, ensures proper segregation of duties, and aligns with best practices observed in mining, oil &amp; gas, and other capital-intensive industries in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.</p>

<h2 id="s2" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">2. Scope &amp; Applicability</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-2" style="color: #374151;">This rule applies to all documents and records that fall under the Finance &amp; Accounting function.</p>

<p class="text-sm font-semibold mb-1" style="color: #081C2E;">Included in IMS (controlled under TE-IMS numbering):</p>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1 mb-3">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span>High-level policies</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span>Strategic procedures and process flows</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span>Approval of workflows and limits of authority</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span>General (non-sensitive) blank forms and templates</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span>Summary-level registers or management dashboards</span></li>
</ul>

<p class="text-sm font-semibold mb-1" style="color: #081C2E;">Excluded from IMS (managed outside TE-IMS numbering):</p>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span>All transactional, detailed, or raw financial records (e.g., invoices, receipts).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span>Any document containing personally identifiable financial information (PII), bank details, tax identifiers, payroll data, or client payment information.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s3" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">3. Rationale — Why Finance &amp; Accounting Requires Special Treatment</h2>

<div class="overflow-x-auto rounded border" style="border-color: #dde3ec;">
  <table class="w-full text-xs border-collapse">
    <thead style="background-color: #081C2E;">
      <tr>
        <th class="px-3 py-2 text-left font-bold uppercase tracking-wider" style="color: #ffffff;">Reason</th>
        <th class="px-3 py-2 text-left font-bold uppercase tracking-wider" style="color: #ffffff;">Explanation</th>
        <th class="px-3 py-2 text-left font-bold uppercase tracking-wider" style="color: #ffffff;">Consequence if not separated</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr style="background-color: rgba(8,28,46,0.03);">
        <td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs font-semibold" style="color: #081C2E;">Data Sensitivity &amp; Confidentiality</td>
        <td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151;">Invoices, bank statements, payroll files, vendor payments, and tax returns contain highly confidential and personally identifiable information (PII) subject to strict data protection rules (e.g., Saudi Personal Data Protection Law – PDPL).</td>
        <td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151;">Risk of unauthorized access, data breaches, or severe non-compliance if mixed with general IMS documents.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs font-semibold" style="color: #081C2E;">Regulatory &amp; Legal Obligations</td>
        <td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151;">ZATCA (Zakat, Tax &amp; Customs Authority), SOCPA, and anti-money laundering regulations require specific retention periods, formats, audit trails, and highly restricted access for financial records.</td>
        <td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151;">Potential fines, negative audit findings, or legal exposure if detailed records are managed under generic IMS rules.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr style="background-color: rgba(8,28,46,0.03);">
        <td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs font-semibold" style="color: #081C2E;">Specialized Software Control</td>
        <td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151;">Most transactional records are generated automatically by the company's accounting/ERP system, which already applies its own secure numbering, versioning, access logs, and immutable audit trails.</td>
        <td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151;">Duplication of effort, version control conflicts, and loss of native software controls if forced into the IMS.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs font-semibold" style="color: #081C2E;">Volume &amp; Frequency</td>
        <td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151;">Finance generates thousands of transactional documents monthly. Forcing all of these into the IMS would make the system unmanageable and dilute its effectiveness.</td>
        <td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151;">IMS becomes bloated, slow, and unusable for its core operational and compliance purpose.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr style="background-color: rgba(8,28,46,0.03);">
        <td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs font-semibold" style="color: #081C2E;">Segregation of Duties</td>
        <td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151;">Only a very limited number of authorized personnel should have access to detailed financial records. IMS documents are designed to be widely accessible to the workforce.</td>
        <td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151;">Breach of segregation of duties and drastically increased insider risk.</td>
      </tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

<h2 id="s5" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">5. Integration Points (How the Two Worlds Connect)</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #374151;">Even though detailed finance records are outside the IMS, the following controlled integration points ensure the IMS framework remains complete:</p>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1 mb-3">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span><strong>Process Linkage:</strong> Finance-related IMS procedures describe the high-level workflow and approval authorities (e.g., "Invoices above SAR 50,000 require Finance Manager + CEO approval").</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span><strong>Data Summaries:</strong> Summary outputs from the finance system (monthly reports, dashboards, exception lists) may be attached or referenced in IMS registers (e.g., Budget Control Register).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span><strong>Performance Metrics:</strong> Finance KPIs (e.g., Days Payable Outstanding, Budget Variance %) are included in the annual IMS Management Review.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span><strong>Audit Evidence:</strong> Audit trails from the finance system are made available to internal/external auditors during IMS audits when required.</span></li>
</ul>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed font-semibold" style="color: #081C2E;">Crucial Rule: No detailed transactional data is ever copied, duplicated, or stored in the IMS repository.</p>

<h2 id="s6" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">6. Access &amp; Security Rules</h2>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span><strong>IMS Documents (Policies/Procedures/Blank Forms):</strong> Normal IMS access rights apply (role-based via SharePoint or equivalent document repository).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span><strong>Detailed Finance Records:</strong> Strictly restricted to authorized Finance personnel only (separate network folder permissions, ERP user roles, no general IMS access).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px; flex-shrink: 0;">•</span><span><strong>Cross-Referencing:</strong> Any IMS document that references a finance system output must use a controlled hyperlink or reference number. Sensitive raw files must never be attached directly to an IMS document.</span></li>
</ul>
