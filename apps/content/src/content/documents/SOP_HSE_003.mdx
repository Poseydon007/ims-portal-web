            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s1" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">1. Purpose</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">To protect all personnel from the unexpected energization, start-up or release of hazardous energy during maintenance, repair, cleaning or servicing of machinery and equipment (including diamond drill rigs). This SOP enforces a Zero Energy State before any such work, preventing injuries and damage to True East Mining and client property.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">This procedure establishes mandatory controls for all energy sources — electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic, mechanical, gravitational, stored, thermal and chemical — in strict compliance with ISO 45001:2018 and KSA occupational safety regulations.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s2" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">2. Scope</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">This procedure applies to all True East Mining employees, contractors and third parties performing maintenance, repair, inspection or servicing on any equipment or machinery (drill rigs, generators, pumps, rod handlers, conveyors, crushers, compressors, etc.) where hazardous energy may be present. It covers all sites, including remote desert drilling operations, and it applies 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, regardless of shift.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s3" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">3. Definitions</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Key terms used in this SOP are defined below for consistency with ISO 45001:2018 and KSA occupational safety regulations.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s4" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">4. Responsibilities</h2>
            </div>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s5" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">5. Energy Sources to be Controlled</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">All forms of hazardous energy at True East Mining sites must be identified and isolated before any servicing work begins. The table below summarizes common sources and the required isolation and verification method.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s6" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">6. Central Lockout Station & Registration</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Central Lockout Station — located in a highly visible, easily accessible area; contains all personal locks, tags, multi-lock hasps, keys, registers and reference documentation.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Registration — every lockout must be formally registered with the Foreman/Supervisor and the Safety Officer BEFORE any lock is applied.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Control — the Foreman controls the station. Unauthorized lockouts are strictly prohibited.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Lockout Register — must be signed by the requester, Foreman and Safety Officer. It is updated continuously to capture date, equipment, reason, personnel involved, and the exact time the lock is applied and removed.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s7" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">7. The 10-Step LOTO Sequence</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Every lockout at True East Mining follows the same 10-step sequence. Steps must be completed in order; none may be skipped. Any deviation triggers an immediate Stop Work and a formal non-conformance report.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s8" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">8. Group Lockout</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Where more than one authorized person works on the same equipment:</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• A multi-lock hasp is applied at the isolation point. Each worker attaches their own personal lock.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• The Isolation Officer maintains the group lockout log showing all lock owners.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Each worker removes their own lock when their portion of the work is complete.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• The equipment is not re-energized until the last personal lock has been removed.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s9" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">9. Lock & Tag Removal Rules</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• ONLY the specific person who applied the lock or tag may remove it — no exceptions under normal conditions.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• If the original person is unavailable (off-shift, emergency, etc.), removal requires: (a) written authorization from the SSE or designated deputy; (b) documented verification that the worker is not in the isolation zone; (c) a formal entry in the Lockout Register; (d) immediate notification to the worker on their return.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Before removing any lock, verify the work is complete, all tools and personnel are clear of the equipment, and guards have been replaced.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Notify all affected employees before re-energization.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s10" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">10. Live Work Exception</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Live work on energized equipment is permitted ONLY where de-energization is physically impossible (for example, live diagnostics on a running generator) and the task is technically justified and pre-approved.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Strict controls for live work:</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• A site-specific JSA/Risk Assessment must be completed and documented.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• A formal Live Work Permit must be signed by the Supervisor and Safety Officer before work begins.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• A strict exclusion zone is established with signage and radio communication.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Mandatory PPE: insulated gloves and tools, FR coveralls, face shield, hydraulic-resistant gloves where pressurized fluid is involved.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• A specific Toolbox Talk is delivered and first-aid readiness confirmed before starting.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Exposure time must be minimized — remote testing is used wherever possible.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Key live-work risks on diamond drill rigs:</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Arc Flash — from generators, main control panels, high-current switchgear.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Hydraulic Injection — from high-pressure fluid lines; injection injuries are surgical emergencies.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Entanglement — from spindle, drill rods, rotating components and rod handler.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Stored Energy — hoisting and rotation systems dropping or releasing unexpectedly when power is restored.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s11" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">11. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Hard hat, safety glasses or goggles and flame-resistant (FR) coveralls.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Insulated gloves and insulated tools (electrical diagnostics).</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Hydraulic-resistant gloves and full face shield (pressurized lines).</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Hearing protection and FFP2 respirators where dust is present in the maintenance area.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Fall protection where working at height above 1.8 m.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s12" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">12. Training and Competency</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• All Isolation Officers, maintenance personnel, and authorized workers must complete initial LOTO training plus an annual refresher.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• The curriculum shall cover: energy identification, isolation techniques, proper lock application and verification, group lockout, Live Work rules and emergency response.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Competency shall be verified through practical assessment before first use of the LOTO system.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• All training records and competency sign-offs shall be retained in the IMS for a minimum of 3 years.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s13" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">13. Records & Retention</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">All LOTO records shall be retained in the IMS archive and made available for ISO 45001 audits and regulatory inspection.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s14" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">14. Monitoring, Audit and Continuous Improvement</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• HSE conducts monthly field audits of the Lockout Register, the physical condition of tags and locks, and field compliance.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• KPIs monitored: percentage of lockouts properly registered; number of LOTO non-conformances; training completion rates.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• This SOP is reviewed annually, immediately following any LOTO-related incident, and upon changes to regulatory standards.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• IMS integration: all LOTO data is linked directly to the Incident Reporting system and the Monthly HSE Report.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s15" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">15. Non-Conformance</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Any deviation from this SOP — including unauthorized lock removal, incomplete isolation, or conducting Live Work without a permit — will trigger:</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• An immediate Stop Work order.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• A formal Incident Report (TE-IMS-FRM-HSE-022).</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Immediate notification to the Site Foreman and Safety Officer.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• A full investigation with formal Corrective Actions (CAPA) and potential disciplinary action per the True East Mining Disciplinary Policy.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s16" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">16. References</h2>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• MHRSD — Occupational Safety and Health Management Regulation (KSA), covering energy control and live work authorization.</p>
