            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s1" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">1. Purpose</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">This SOP establishes safe, standardised procedures for refueling equipment and refilling bulk fuel storage at True East Mining Company (TEMC) sites. It prevents fires, explosions, static discharge, spills, environmental contamination, and injuries, and ensures compliance with ISO 45001:2018, ISO 14001:2015, MHRSD, NCEC, and Saudi environmental and safety regulations.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s2" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">2. Scope</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">This SOP applies to all refueling and refilling activities involving diesel, gasoline, and other fuels at TEMC operational sites — drilling rigs, generators, light vehicles, heavy equipment, and bulk fuel storage. It binds all employees, contractors, and third-party fuel suppliers operating on company premises.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s3" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">3. Definitions</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Refueling — transferring fuel directly from a source into the active tank of equipment or a vehicle.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Refilling — transferring fuel from a delivery bowser into bulk storage drums or static tanks on site.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Minor Spill — unintended release of ≤ 20 litres of fuel — contained and cleaned on site.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Major Spill — unintended release of &gt; 20 litres — reportable to NCEC, requires formal incident investigation.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Bunded Containment — secondary spill-containment structure around fuel handling or storage.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Grounding / Bonding — electrical connection between bowser and receiving tank to prevent static discharge.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Hot Work — any activity generating ignition sources — welding, grinding, cutting, flame.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s4" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">4. Responsibilities</h2>
            </div>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s5" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">5. Pre-Refueling Preparation</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Risk assessment — dynamic risk assessment before starting — ignition sources, spill potential, weather, environmental proximity, ventilation.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Equipment shutdown — switch off engines completely before opening any fuel cap. No refueling with engine running.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Fire safety — fully charged fire extinguisher (minimum 9 kg dry chemical ABC) within 5 m of the refueling point, accessible.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Volume limits — maximum 4 × 201 litre drums on the active site at any one time to limit spill exposure.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Position — level, firm ground; away from surface drains, wadis, and camp living areas; upwind of ignition sources.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s6" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">6. Safe Refueling Steps</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Containment setup — drip trays directly under hose connections and transfer points.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Bunding — sand wall or portable PVC bund around drip trays; heavy-duty plastic liner below for secondary containment.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Bowser delivery — a designated spotter guides the bowser to a safe, level position. Bowser engine OFF (unless running PTO pump). Wheel chocks applied.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Grounding and bonding — bonding cable connected between bowser and receiving tank before flow begins, to eliminate static discharge.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Ignition control — no smoking, no mobile phone use, no hot work within 10 m. No electrical switching of non-intrinsically-safe equipment in the zone.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Slow fill — begin flow slowly to avoid splash charging. Do not top off past the fill mark.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Weather protection — drums sealed immediately after filling to prevent rain or dust ingress.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Completion — replace and firmly secure all caps and lids. Disconnect grounding last.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s7" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">7. Spill Response</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Immediate action: stop flow, isolate source, deploy spill kit and absorbents.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Minor spills (&lt; 20 L): contain with absorbent, remove contaminated soil/absorbents with a spark-proof spade, place in the designated hazardous waste drum (black carbon).</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Major spills (&gt; 20 L): stop the operation entirely. Notify Supervisor and HSE immediately. Contain the spill. Report per NCEC requirements and PROC-HSE-006 Incident Investigation.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Personnel evacuate upwind. No hot work, switching, or ignition activity until the area is confirmed safe and vapours have cleared.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Waste is transferred to an approved hazardous waste contractor with manifest.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s8" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">8. Post-Refueling</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Inspection: check the entire area and beneath the equipment for residual drips or spills.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Housekeeping: return extinguishers, drip trays, bunds, and grounding cables to designated storage.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Documentation: record volume, equipment, operator, and any issues in the Fuel Refueling Log (TE-IMS-FRM-MAINT-003 or REG-MAINT-002).</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s9" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">9. Personal Protective Equipment</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Personnel actively transferring fuel wear the following minimum PPE:</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Safety glasses or chemical splash goggles.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Chemical-resistant gloves (heavy-duty nitrile).</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Hard hat.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Flame-resistant (FR) overalls.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Steel-toe safety boots.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• High-visibility vest.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s10" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">10. Training and Awareness</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Mandatory formal training at induction + annual refresher for all personnel involved in refueling.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Curriculum covers: hazards of fuel, static and ignition control, bonding and grounding, spill response, PPE, fire safety, and environmental reporting.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Competency verified by the HSE Manager or delegate before first use.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Records retained in the IMS Training Register (REG-TRN-001).</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s11" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">11. Monitoring and Continual Improvement</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Monthly HSE audits of active refueling areas, bulk storage setups, and the Fuel Refueling Log.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Monthly review of spill events, volumes, and compliance rate.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Annual review of this SOP or immediately after any significant spill or fire incident.</p>
