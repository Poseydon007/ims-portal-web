            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s1" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">1. Purpose</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">This SOP establishes a standardised process for conducting Planned Task Observations (PTOs) across all True East Mining Company (TEMC) drilling and exploration operations. PTOs evaluate the safety, efficiency, and environmental compliance of critical and routine tasks, verify that safe work procedures are being followed, reinforce positive behaviour, and drive continual improvement. The SOP supports ISO 45001:2018 (Clauses 6.1.2, 8.1, 9.1) and ISO 14001:2015 (Clause 8.1), and aligns with KSA MHRSD and client HSE requirements.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s2" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">2. Scope</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">This SOP applies to all TEMC employees, contractors, and visitors performing work at TEMC-controlled sites — drill rigs, exploration camps, workshops, core yards, fuel storage areas, and offices. It covers planned task observations of both high-risk and routine tasks, both on TEMC and on client/host sites where TEMC personnel are present.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s3" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">3. Definitions</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Planned Task Observation (PTO) — a structured observation of a task by a trained observer to verify safe, efficient, and compliant execution.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Observer — a competent and trained person conducting the PTO — supervisor, HSE officer, or nominated senior operator.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Critical Task — a task with high safety, environmental, or operational risk — e.g. hoisting, handling drill pipe, working at height, hot work, confined space entry, chemical transfer, refuelling, heavy equipment operation.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Routine Task — a recurring low-to-moderate risk task — e.g. general maintenance, material handling, housekeeping.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Safe Act — behaviour that complies with the procedure, JSA, permit, or safe work practice.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• At-Risk Behaviour — behaviour that increases injury, environmental, or asset-damage risk.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• JSA / SWP — Job Safety Analysis / Safe Work Procedure — the documented safe method for the task.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s4" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">4. Responsibilities</h2>
            </div>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s5" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">5. Planning and Preparation</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• 5.1 Define Objectives — verify compliance with JSA/SWP, detect unsafe or at-risk behaviour, reinforce safe practice, identify training or procedure gaps, feed continuous improvement.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• 5.2 Select Tasks — prioritise critical tasks (hoisting, pipe handling, working at height, hot work, confined space, refuelling, chemical transfer, heavy equipment); rotate routine tasks to ensure coverage.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• 5.3 Schedule — Site HSE Manager issues the monthly PTO schedule to observers — minimum one PTO per supervisor per month, additional PTOs after incidents, for new procedures, or for new hires.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• 5.4 Select Observers — trained and competent in the task, JSA, and the PTO technique; independent from daily direct supervision of the observee where practical.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• 5.5 Prepare — observer reviews the JSA, applicable permit, procedure, and recent incident/audit findings. Uses the PTO form (FRM-HSE-007) and the task-specific checklist covering safety (PPE, LOTO, hazard control), health (ergonomics, dust, noise, chemicals), environment (waste, spill, emissions), and procedure compliance.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s6" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">6. Conducting the Observation</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• 6.1 Pre-brief — observer introduces themselves to the worker(s) and supervisor, explains the PTO is for safety improvement not discipline, confirms the task to be performed.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• 6.2 Observe — observe the task in real time without interfering unless an immediate hazard is identified. If an immediate hazard is seen, exercise Stop Work Authority — stop the job, correct the condition, then allow work to resume.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• 6.3 Record — note safe acts and at-risk behaviours against the checklist — PPE use, JSA compliance, hazard awareness, tool and equipment use, environmental practice.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• 6.4 Feedback — immediately recognise safe behaviour, address at-risk behaviour constructively with the worker on the spot, coach on the correct method.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• 6.5 Evidence — photographs or sketches where permitted and useful; no photographs in client-restricted or sensitive areas without prior approval.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s7" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">7. Reporting and Classification</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• The observer completes the PTO Form (FRM-HSE-007) with task details, findings (safe acts and at-risk behaviours), corrective actions, and recommended improvements.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Findings are classified as Critical / Major / Minor per the table below.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Completed PTO forms submitted to the Site HSE Manager within 24 hours.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Critical findings escalated immediately; NCR raised under PROC-SYS-008 Corrective Action.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s8" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">8. Corrective Actions and Follow-Up</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Every Major and Critical finding becomes a Corrective Action Request (FRM-SYS-001) with owner and target date.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Minor findings handled through on-the-spot coaching and logged for trend analysis.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• The Site HSE Manager tracks corrective actions to closure on the Corrective Action Log.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Follow-up observations verify the effectiveness of corrective actions.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Repeat non-compliance triggers root cause analysis under PROC-SYS-008 and refresher training under PROC-TRN-001.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s9" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">9. Records and Integration</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• PTO forms, checklists, and corrective action records are stored in the HSE Management System and retained for ≥3 years (5 years for Critical findings).</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Findings are reported in the Monthly HSE Report to the SSE.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• PTO trend data feeds: toolbox talk topics, risk assessments and JSA updates, training refresher topics, and management review inputs.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Trends are shared with contractors operating on TEMC sites to drive site-wide HSE performance.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s10" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">10. Continual Improvement</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• The Site HSE Manager reviews PTO data monthly for recurring issues, training needs, and JSA weaknesses.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Checklists and PTO forms are reviewed annually and after any significant incident, regulatory change, or new task type.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Annual PTO programme effectiveness is reviewed as an input to the Management Review (PROC-SYS-003).</p>
