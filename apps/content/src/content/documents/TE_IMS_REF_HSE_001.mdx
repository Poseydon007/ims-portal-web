<h2 id="s1" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">1. Purpose</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;">To establish and maintain procedures to identify potential foreseeable accidents and emergency situations, to prevent and control Environment and Safety associated impacts, and to test effectiveness, review, and revise such procedures periodically.</p>

<h2 id="s2" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">2. Scope</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;">This procedure is applicable to any fire, explosion, or other disaster leading to an emergency situation — meaning any significant, non-routine situation which endangers personnel, property, other interested parties, or surrounding environments.</p>

<h2 id="s3" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">3. Responsibility</h2>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1">
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Management Representative is responsible for effective implementation of the disaster management plan.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Functional Heads are responsible for implementation of this procedure in emergency situations.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s4" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">4. Definitions</h2>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1">
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>EMS — Environment Management System</span></li>

  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>MR — Management Representative</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s6" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">6. Procedure</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #374151;">Management Representative shall prepare a disaster management plan (emergency preparedness) for on-site emergencies, clearly detailing responsibilities for each identified potential emergency situation. While preparing the plan, consider environmental and safety impacts associated with the activity.</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #374151;">Identification of potential accident and emergency situations is carried out during the initial EMS and H&amp;SM review. The potential emergencies and areas identified are as follows:</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-4" style="color: #374151;">The prevention, mitigation, and corrective and preventive actions are detailed below for each type of foreseeable potential emergency.</p>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-4 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">Fire — Prevention</h3>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1 mb-4">
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Keep fire extinguishers well maintained.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Ensure that training regarding use of firefighting equipment is provided to concerned employees and security guards.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Maintain and follow Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS) for all chemicals used in the company including finished products.</span></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-4 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">Fire — Mitigation</h3>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1 mb-4">
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Any person noticing fire shall pass on the information through phone or messenger to Security personnel at the main gate and the concerned employee.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Use suitable firefighting equipment (fire extinguishers).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>If fire cannot be extinguished or has erupted in a large way, take help from nearby industries and the local firefighting team.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Cordon off the area and do not allow people to come near the fire.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Switch off electricity near the place of fire.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Take appropriate action for mitigation of environmental impact due to fire.</span></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-4 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">Corrective and Preventive Action</h3>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1 mb-4">
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Analyze the causes of the fire and prepare a report specifying measures to prevent recurrence.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Document corrective and preventive actions in the corresponding applicable procedures.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Communicate about the incident to the regulatory authorities in the prescribed form.</span></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-4 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">Occupational Accidents</h3>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1 mb-4">
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Electrical shocks</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Slips, Trips, Falls, and Accidents (STFA)</span></li>
</ul>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-4" style="color: #374151;">Management Representative shall carry out yearly mock drills of accident or emergency situations where practicable, and keep records.</p>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-4 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">Review and Disposition</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;">The on-site emergency plan shall be reviewed and revised once a year and also in light of any findings. Changes shall be maintained. Investigation and maintenance of accident records shall be conducted as per procedure.</p>
