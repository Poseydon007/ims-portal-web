<h2 id="s1" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">1. Purpose</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">This procedure establishes and maintains a systematic process to proactively identify hazards across all True East Mining Company (TEMC) activities, assess the associated occupational health, safety, and environmental risks, and implement controls to reduce those risks to a tolerable or acceptable level.</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">It satisfies the requirements of ISO 45001:2018 Clauses 6.1.2 (Hazard Identification and Assessment of OH&amp;S Risks) and 8.1.2 (Eliminating Hazards and Reducing OH&amp;S Risks), ISO 14001:2015 Clause 6.1.2 (Environmental Aspects), and ISO 9001:2015 Clause 6.1 (Actions to Address Risks and Opportunities). It also supports TEMC's compliance with the KSA Ministry of Human Resources and Social Development (MHRSD) occupational safety regulations, the Saudi Labour Law (Royal Decree M/51), Ma'aden contractor HSE standards, and applicable National Center for Environmental Compliance (NCEC) requirements.</p>

<h2 id="s2" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">2. Scope</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">This procedure applies to all TEMC activities, personnel, contractors, visitors, and third parties working on TEMC-controlled sites or on TEMC's behalf at client-controlled sites. It covers:</p>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Drilling operations — RC, DTH, diamond core, and water well drilling.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Exploration camps, core yards, workshops, logistics yards, fuel storage, and offices.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Mobilisation and demobilisation activities, site preparation, and road works.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Routine and non-routine maintenance, including hot work and confined space.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Client-controlled areas where TEMC personnel are deployed (e.g. Ma'aden sites).</span></li>
</ul>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Risk assessments are carried out:</p>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>At the planning stage of any new project, programme, campaign, or mobilisation.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Before commencement of any non-routine activity.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Whenever there is a significant change in scope, equipment, material, method, site, personnel, or regulation.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Following any incident, near miss, or significant finding from an inspection or audit.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>At planned review intervals (minimum annually for active risk assessments).</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s5" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">5. Risk Assessment Procedure</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">A workplace risk assessment is conducted whenever there is a significant change in the work programme, an obvious work hazard that needs to be managed, a new activity being planned, or a trigger as defined in Section 2. The assessment is conducted by a multi-disciplinary RA Team and recorded on FRM-HSE-001 Risk Assessment Form. The results are approved by the Operations Manager and HSE Manager before the activity proceeds. High and Critical rated residual risks require additional approvals as set out in Section 5.4.</p>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">5.1 Step 1 — Identify the Hazards and Existing Controls</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Hazards are identified through a systematic approach combining the following sources:</p>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Workplace inspections ("Walk the Job") covering every step of the activity from start to finish.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Review of the task schedule, method statement, equipment list, and material safety data sheets.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Review of incident reports, near-miss logs, audit findings, and previous risk assessments for similar activities.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Review of applicable KSA regulations, ISO standards, and client HSE requirements (e.g. Ma'aden Code of Practice).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Consultation with the workers who perform the task — they know where it actually goes wrong.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>The Hazard Identification Prompt Checklist (Appendix B, issued separately as FRM-HSE-010).</span></li>
</ul>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Consideration is given to reasonably foreseeable changes in circumstances, abnormal conditions, emergencies, and degraded modes of operation. Both routine and non-routine activities are assessed.</p>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">5.2 Step 2 — Assess the Severity of Consequences</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Severity is the worst credible outcome of the hazardous event, considering harm to personnel (employee, contractor, public), damage to property and assets, damage to the environment, and business impact. Severity is scored from 1 to 5 using the following scale:</p>

<div class="overflow-x-auto mb-6 rounded border" style="border-color: #dde3ec;">
  <table class="w-full text-sm border-collapse">
    <thead style="background-color: #081C2E;">
      <tr>
        <th class="px-3 py-2 text-left text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider" style="color: #ffffff;">Level</th>
        <th class="px-3 py-2 text-left text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider" style="color: #ffffff;">Severity Description</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr style="background-color: rgba(8,28,46,0.03);"><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">1 — Insignificant</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">No injury; negligible damage; no environmental effect; no business impact. First-aid not required.</td></tr>
      <tr><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">2 — Minor</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">First-aid injury; minor property damage; easily cleaned spill contained on site; minor schedule delay.</td></tr>
      <tr style="background-color: rgba(8,28,46,0.03);"><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">3 — Moderate</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">Medical treatment case; moderate asset damage; reportable environmental release; localised operational impact.</td></tr>
      <tr><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">4 — Major</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">Lost-time injury or serious injury; significant asset damage; major environmental release requiring NCEC notification; reportable to MHRSD under Saudi Labour Law Art. 142 and Royal Decree M/51.</td></tr>
      <tr style="background-color: rgba(8,28,46,0.03);"><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">5 — Severe</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">Single or multiple fatality, or permanent disability; catastrophic asset loss; long-term or irreversible environmental damage; loss of licence to operate; national-level media and regulatory impact.</td></tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">5.3 Step 3 — Assess the Likelihood of Occurrence</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Likelihood is the probability that the hazardous event will occur and cause harm, taking into account frequency of exposure, effectiveness of existing controls, number of people exposed, and environmental conditions. Likelihood is scored from 1 to 5 using the following scale:</p>

<div class="overflow-x-auto mb-6 rounded border" style="border-color: #dde3ec;">
  <table class="w-full text-sm border-collapse">
    <thead style="background-color: #081C2E;">
      <tr>
        <th class="px-3 py-2 text-left text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider" style="color: #ffffff;">Level</th>
        <th class="px-3 py-2 text-left text-xs font-bold uppercase tracking-wider" style="color: #ffffff;">Likelihood Description</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr style="background-color: rgba(8,28,46,0.03);"><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">1 — Not Likely</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">Postulated event. May be theoretically possible but has never occurred at TEMC or within the wider industry.</td></tr>
      <tr><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">2 — Low</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">Has occurred rarely. Known to have happened within the industry but not at TEMC. Statistically infrequent.</td></tr>
      <tr style="background-color: rgba(8,28,46,0.03);"><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">3 — Likely</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">Has occurred infrequently — less than once per year. Credible that it may recur within three years.</td></tr>
      <tr><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">4 — Highly Likely</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">Has occurred frequently — once or more per year. Likely to recur within a year if controls are not strengthened.</td></tr>
      <tr style="background-color: rgba(8,28,46,0.03);"><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">5 — Frequent</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #081C2E;">Very frequent exposure. Event has occurred multiple times or is expected during normal operations.</td></tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Note: Assessors must not rely on "common sense" alone — if a task can be done unsafely, sooner or later it will be. Likelihood is assessed with honest regard to actual field conditions, not idealised ones.</p>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">5.4 Step 4 — Calculate the Risk Rating</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">The risk rating is the product of severity and likelihood:</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Risk Rating  =  Severity  ×  Likelihood</p>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">5.5 Step 5 — Select Additional Controls Using the Hierarchy of Controls</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">If the inherent risk rating requires additional controls, they are selected using the Hierarchy of Controls. The most effective methods must be considered first. Lower-level controls (Administrative, PPE) are only relied on when higher-level controls are not reasonably practicable, and should be combined wherever possible.</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Important: Ensure that any new control measure does not introduce another hazard. Controls must be verifiable, implementable, and clearly assigned to an owner with a target date.</p>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">5.6 Step 6 — Determine Residual Risk and Action Plan</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">The selected additional controls are recorded on FRM-HSE-001 and the residual risk rating is calculated using the same matrix as Step 4. The residual risk must be reduced to LOW or, at worst, MEDIUM with contingency in place before the activity proceeds. Each additional control measure is assigned an owner, target closure date, and verification method.</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">The principle of ALARP applies: risks must be reduced as low as reasonably practicable. Further reduction is pursued unless the cost, time, and effort required are grossly disproportionate to the benefit gained.</p>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">5.7 Step 7 — Implementation, Monitoring, and Review</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Controls are implemented through one or more of the following mechanisms, as appropriate:</p>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>New or revised procedures, codes of practice, or safe systems of work.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Changes to the workplace layout, equipment, or guarding.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>New equipment selection or modification.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Targeted training, toolbox talks, or competency assessment.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Increased supervision, permits, or monitoring.</span></li>
</ul>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Every risk assessment is reviewed:</p>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>At least annually for all active risk assessments.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>After any incident or near-miss related to the activity.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>After a significant process, equipment, personnel, site, or regulatory change.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>When the closure date of a control measure is reached, to verify effectiveness.</span></li>
</ul>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">The outcome of each review is recorded on FRM-HSE-001 and reflected in the HIRA Register (REG-HSE-007). Closed items are retained in the register as the historical record.</p>

<h2 id="s10" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">10. Appendix A — Activities Requiring a Risk Assessment</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">The following activities must always be covered by a documented risk assessment (FRM-HSE-001). This list is non-exhaustive — any activity with credible potential for harm shall be assessed.</p>

  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Rod handling, core tray handling, sample bagging and transport.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Drill pad preparation, track building, and site restoration.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Mud mixing, sump construction, and handling of drilling additives.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Wireline operations and downhole tool handling.</span></li>
