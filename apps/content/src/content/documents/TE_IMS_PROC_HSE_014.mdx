<h2 id="s1" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">1. Purpose</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">This procedure establishes the requirements for the safe, effective, and auditable rehabilitation of all True East Mining Company (TEMC) drilling and exploration sites on completion of activities. The objective is to return disturbed land as close as practicable to its pre-disturbance condition, prevent soil erosion, groundwater contamination, and habitat loss, and ensure full compliance with ISO 14001:2015, ISO 45001:2018, MEWA, NCEC, and the Saudi Mineral Investment Law and its Implementing Regulations.</p>

<h2 id="s2" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">2. Scope</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">This procedure applies to all TEMC exploration and drilling sites in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, including drill pads, sumps, sample storage areas, access tracks, gridlines, camps, and temporary yards. It covers employees, contractors, and subcontractors involved in planning, executing, and verifying rehabilitation. Site-specific rehabilitation plans may add further requirements where host-licence conditions demand them.</p>

<h2 id="s3" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">3. Definitions</h2>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Rehabilitation — the process of restoring disturbed land to a stable, safe, and environmentally acceptable condition.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Pre-disturbance Baseline — the documented condition of the site before any disturbance, captured via photos, GPS coordinates, soil and vegetation notes.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Drill Hole Abandonment — the permanent sealing and identification of a drill hole in compliance with regulatory and environmental standards.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Sump — an excavated pit used during drilling to manage drilling fluids, cuttings, or water.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Topsoil — the upper 0.1–0.3 m of soil containing organic matter and seed bank; preserved separately during disturbance for re-spreading.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Contaminated Cuttings — drill cuttings that are acidic, saline, radioactive, or chemically altered such that they cannot be safely dispersed.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Completion Report — the formal record confirming rehabilitation is complete, signed by HSE Manager and accepted by Site Manager.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s5" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">5. Pre-Rehabilitation Planning</h2>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Regulatory review — confirm obligations under the current exploration licence, NCEC guidelines, MEWA requirements, and Saudi Mineral Investment Law Implementing Regulations.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Stakeholder consultation — engage host licence area representatives, landowners, and local authorities where applicable.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Baseline documentation — photograph each disturbed location from fixed GPS points. Capture vegetation cover and soil profile notes. Records stored in the site rehabilitation file.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Rehabilitation plan — site-specific plan covering scope, sequence, resources, verification steps, responsible persons, and target completion date.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Risk assessment — identify immediate hazards — unstable ground, open holes, sharp objects, contaminated zones, residual chemicals — and define controls before entry.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Mobilisation — confirm equipment, fuel, PPE, and waste-handling capacity before work starts.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s6" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">6. Drill Hole Abandonment</h2>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Remove all drill rods, liners, and temporary casing before sealing.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Classify drill cuttings — contaminated (acidic, saline, radioactive, discoloured) vs non-contaminated — before disposal decisions.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Return contaminated cuttings to the hole, sump, or an approved lined excavation. Do not disperse.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Non-contaminated cuttings may be raked over the area and used for contouring.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Prevent aquifer cross-contamination — seal hole at aquifer transitions where encountered.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Plug the hole with cement in sections per licence conditions. Insert a PVC plug 0.5–1.0 m below surface where plastic plugging is allowed.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Embed a permanent ID marker showing hole ID, total depth, and abandonment date.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Steel casing that cannot be removed shall be cut ≥0.5 m below surface and capped or welded shut — never left protruding.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s7" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">7. Site Rehabilitation Execution</h2>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Sumps — backfill with stored excavated material, respread topsoil, and restore surface contour.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Access tracks and gridlines — rip compacted ground along natural contour lines (not downslope) to loosen soil, promote infiltration, and reduce erosion. Restore natural drainage.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Topsoil and overburden — respread evenly across the disturbed footprint to re-establish seed bank conditions.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Survey markers — minimise permanent markers. Use biodegradable wooden pegs instead of steel stakes wherever licence conditions allow.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Waste removal — 100% removal of sample bags, packaging, plastic, scrap metal, hydrocarbon-contaminated soil, and general waste. Dispose per TEMC Waste Management Procedure and NCEC requirements.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Hydrocarbon impact — excavate visibly contaminated soil, contain, and dispose through licensed contractor. Record quantity removed.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Revegetation — where the licence requires active revegetation, apply approved native seed mix; monitor germination at 3, 6, and 12 months.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Final inspection — HSE team walks the site, verifies compliance, re-photographs from baseline GPS points, and documents findings in the Rehabilitation Completion Report.</span></li>
</ul>
