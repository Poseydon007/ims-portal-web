<h2 id="s1" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">1. Purpose</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">To minimise the risk of musculoskeletal injuries (MSIs) and incidents associated with manual handling tasks at True East Mining sites. This is achieved by establishing safe practices, targeted training, dynamic task-level risk assessments and mandating mechanical aids wherever practical, in compliance with ISO 45001:2018 and KSA MHRSD occupational safety regulations.</p>

<h2 id="s2" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">2. Scope</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">This procedure applies to all employees, contractors, sub-contractors and visitors performing manual handling tasks (lifting, lowering, pushing, pulling, carrying, moving) at drilling sites, processing areas, workshops, laydown yards, camps and remote desert locations. It explicitly covers heavy or awkward items such as drill rods, wet core boxes, hand tools, cement and drilling-additive bags, water drums and operational equipment.</p>

<h2 id="s5" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">5. Risk Factors</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Personnel must remain highly vigilant of the following manual handling risk factors present on True East Mining sites:</p>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Heavy, awkward or unstable loads — long drill rods, water-logged core boxes, cement sacks, coiled hoses.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Repetitive handling or tasks requiring awkward, twisting or bent-back postures.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Uneven, rocky or slippery ground — desert terrain, muddy rig mats, snow of drill cuttings.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Extreme high temperatures — rapid fatigue, sweaty palms, reduced grip strength (cross-refer PROC-HSE-002 Heat Stress).</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Long carrying distances or areas with poor lighting (night shift operations).</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Pre-existing musculoskeletal conditions or recent injuries.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Inadequate PPE — unsuitable gloves, missing metatarsal protection.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Time pressure, rushed work or understaffed crews.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s6" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">6. Procedure</h2>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.1 Hierarchy of Controls (Avoid → Reduce → Assess → Train)</h3>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Avoid — avoid manual handling entirely where possible by using mechanical aids — cranes, trolleys, forklifts, rod handlers, core box lifters.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Reduce Risk — if manual handling cannot be avoided, break loads into smaller units, redesign the task to eliminate bending, or mandate a team lift.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Assess — assess every task before handling by asking the questions in Section 6.2.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Train — train all personnel on safe handling — initial induction plus annual refresher.</span></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.2 Pre-Lift Assessment Checklist (Dynamic)</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Before any significant lift, every person must ask themselves:</p>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Can a mechanical aid be used instead?</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Can the load be reduced, dismantled or split?</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Does the job require a team lift?</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Is the pathway clear, well-lit and free of trip hazards?</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Is the final stacking area safe — stable ground, safe height, proper packing?</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Am I physically capable of this lift today — rested, hydrated, injury-free?</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Is my PPE appropriate for the load?</span></li>
</ul>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">If the answer to any of these raises concern, the lift must not proceed without additional controls. Stop Work Authority applies.</p>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.3 Safe Manual Handling Technique</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Always plan the lift before touching the object:</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Step 1: Assess — know the load weight — read the label, ask a supervisor, or gently test it.</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Step 2: Stance — stand close to the load with feet apart (shoulder-width, approx. 300 mm) for a stable base.</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Step 3: Posture — bend at the knees and hips. Keep your back straight and chin tucked.</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Step 4: Grip — get a firm full-palm grip. Use designed handles if available. Do not rely on fingertips alone.</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Step 5: Brace — breathe in and tighten your core just before lifting — inflated lungs and tense core help support the spine.</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Step 6: Lift — lift smoothly using your powerful leg muscles. Keep the load as close to your body as possible.</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Step 7: Move — never twist your spine. If you need to turn, turn by moving your feet.</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Step 8: Lower — lower the object slowly using your legs, not your back.</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Step 9: Visibility — if your view is obstructed by the load, get help or use a dedicated spotter.</p>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.4 Indicative Weight Limits (Single Person, Healthy Adult)</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">These are guidance values only — actual limits must be adjusted for individual capability, posture, duration, frequency, grip quality, environmental conditions and heat stress. When in doubt, use a mechanical aid or team lift.</p>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.5 Team Lifting</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">When a load is too heavy or awkward for one person:</p>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Nominate a Leader — one person must be nominated to call the commands.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Coordinate — lift, move and lower exactly together.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Balance — ensure an equal share of the load based on height and strength.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Communicate — use clear, loud commands — 'Ready… Lift!', 'Lower… Now'.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Match Heights — team members should be of similar height where possible; if not, distribute load to compensate.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Stop and Reset — if any member loses grip, balance or control, the leader must call an immediate stop.</span></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.6 PPE and Equipment</h3>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Wear cut-resistant impact gloves appropriate to the load.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Steel-toe boots with metatarsal protection.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>High-visibility reflective overalls and hard hat.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Back support belts are NOT mandated by TE (evidence on effectiveness is limited); may be used where individually prescribed by occupational health.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Use mechanical aids — trolleys, dollies, hoists, rod handlers, forklifts — whenever available and you are authorised/trained to use them.</span></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.7 Training and Awareness</h3>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Mandatory manual handling training during initial induction plus annual refresher.</span></li>
</ul>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Task-specific training for rod handling, core box handling and cement/additive handling.</span></li>
