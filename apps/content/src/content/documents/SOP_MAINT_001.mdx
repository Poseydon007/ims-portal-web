            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s1" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">1. Purpose</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">This SOP sets the minimum standard for the installation, inspection, maintenance, and control of machine guarding on all rotating, reciprocating, or moving equipment at True East Mining Company (TEMC). Its objective is to prevent contact injuries — crushing, entanglement, shearing, amputation, ejection, and abrasion — and to meet ISO 45001:2018 (Clause 8.1.2), MHRSD occupational safety regulations, and OEM design requirements.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s2" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">2. Scope</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">This SOP applies to all fixed, mobile, and portable equipment operated by TEMC or its contractors — drill rig rotating components, top drives, draw works, mud pumps, generators, compressors, grinders, drill press, belt and chain drives, fans, pulleys, sheaves, crushers, workshop tools, and any equipment where a person could contact a moving part.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s3" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">3. Definitions</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Machine Guard — a physical barrier that prevents access to dangerous moving parts during normal operation.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Fixed Guard — bolted or welded; removal requires tools.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Interlocked Guard — connected to the machine control — removal or opening stops the machine.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Adjustable Guard — positioned to suit the workpiece (e.g. drill press).</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Self-Adjusting Guard — moves with the workpiece and returns automatically.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Pinch Point — location where two or more parts move in such a way a body part could be caught.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Nip Point — point where rotating parts meet (rollers, belt-and-pulley, chain-and-sprocket).</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• LOTO — Lockout / Tagout — energy isolation procedure per SOP-HSE-003.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s4" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">4. Responsibilities</h2>
            </div>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s5" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">5. Minimum Guarding Requirements</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• All rotating shafts, drives, gears, belts, chains, sprockets, pulleys, flywheels, and couplings within 2.1 m of the work surface are guarded.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Guards meet or exceed OEM specifications — material, dimensions, fixings, clearances.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Fastened with secure bolts or purpose-designed quick-release devices — never wire, tape, or zip ties.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Allow safe lubrication, inspection, and adjustment without full removal where possible.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Do not create new hazards (trap points, sharp edges).</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Clearly identified — yellow or safety colour where visibility is required.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Interlocked guards tested to confirm they stop the machine when opened.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s6" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">6. Pre-Use Inspection (Daily)</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Operators and supervisors verify before each shift or task:</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• All required guards are present and correctly positioned.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Guards are secure — no loose bolts, cracks, deformation, or corrosion.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Interlocks function — the machine will not start with the guard open.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• No modification, bypass, or damage.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Warning signs and labels are legible.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Recorded on the Daily Equipment Checklist or guarded-equipment inspection line.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s7" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">7. Formal Inspection (Monthly)</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Maintenance conducts a documented monthly inspection of all guarded equipment using the Machine Guarding Register (TE-IMS-FRM-MAINT-001).</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Inspection covers structural condition, fasteners, interlocks, clearances, signage, and OEM compliance.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Findings classified: OK / Minor (repair within 7 days) / Major (equipment taken Out of Service until repaired).</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Corrective actions tracked to closure.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s8" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">8. Removal and Reinstatement</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Authorisation — guards may only be removed under an approved Work Order and after LOTO (SOP-HSE-003).</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Barrier / tag — the work area is barricaded and the equipment tagged Out of Service while the guard is off.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Reinstatement — guards are reinstalled exactly per OEM — correct fixings, torque, position — before power is restored.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Function test — operator or competent person verifies guard is secure and (where applicable) interlock tested before release.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Sign-off — the Maintenance Supervisor signs off the Work Order confirming guard is in place and functional.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s9" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">9. Prohibited Practices</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Operating equipment with any guard missing, damaged, loose, or bypassed.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Modifying a guard without written approval of the Maintenance Manager and HSE.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Reaching past, under, or over a guard while machinery is running.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Defeating an interlock by any means — mechanical wedge, electrical jumper, cable tie.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Wearing loose clothing, gloves, jewellery, or long hair unrestrained near rotating parts.</p>

            <div class="mb-4 pb-3 mt-8" style="border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">
              <h2 id="s10" class="text-lg font-bold" style="color: #081C2E;">10. Training</h2>
            </div>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Operators and maintenance personnel trained in guarding principles, hazards, inspection, and LOTO at induction + annual refresher.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Task-specific training for each piece of guarded equipment they will operate or service.</p>

            <p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">• Records held in the Training and Competence Matrix (TE-IMS-REG-TRN-001).</p>
