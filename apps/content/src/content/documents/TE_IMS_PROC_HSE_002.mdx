<h2 id="s1" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">1. Purpose</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;">To minimise the risk of heat-related illnesses among personnel working in hot environments at True East Mining exploration drilling sites in Saudi Arabia. This procedure establishes targeted training, engineering and administrative controls, hydration and shade provisions, continuous WBGT monitoring and rapid emergency response — ensuring a safe and productive workplace during peak summer operations.</p>

<h2 id="s2" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">2. Scope</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;">This procedure applies to all employees, contractors and visitors exposed to high ambient temperatures and/or heavy physical work at True East Mining sites. It is particularly critical during outdoor drilling, rig setup, rod handling, core retrieval and throughout the summer months (April–October) when the WBGT routinely exceeds 30 °C. It integrates with the Fatigue Management Procedure (TE-IMS-PROC-HSE-001) and the Site Emergency Preparedness Procedure (TE-IMS-PROC-HSE-004).</p>

<h2 id="s5" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">5. Risk Factors</h2>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>High ambient temperatures and extreme humidity — Saudi desert summer conditions, WBGT routinely &gt;30 °C between May and September.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Heavy physical work: rigging, manual rod handling, heavy lifting, core handling.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Heavy, restrictive PPE: flame-resistant overalls, gloves, hard hats, eye protection.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Long shifts and limited natural shade at exploration sites.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Ramadan fasting — severely reduced daytime fluid and caloric intake.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Remote drilling sites with delayed access to advanced medical facilities.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Pre-existing medical conditions: hypertension, cardiovascular disease, diabetes, recent illness, certain medications.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Inadequate sleep or prior-day dehydration carrying over to next shift.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>New, returning or non-acclimatised workers (first 7–14 days).</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s6" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">6. Procedure</h2>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.1 Training and Awareness</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #374151;">All personnel must receive mandatory heat stress training: initial induction plus annual summer refresher. Topics cover:</p>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Environmental and personal risk factors causing heat-related illnesses.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Critical importance of drinking frequent, small quantities of water — minimum 4 litres per 10-hour shift, scaling up to 6–8 litres in extreme heat.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Recognising signs and symptoms of heat disorders and the buddy-system of monitoring.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Procedures for reporting symptoms and activating emergency services (999 / 997 in KSA).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>The physiological acclimatisation process and its timing.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Ramadan-specific hydration strategies and Suhoor/Iftar planning.</span></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.2 Supervisor Responsibilities</h3>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Brief the crew on this procedure before assigning heavy work in extreme heat.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Monitor the WBGT daily using a portable site meter or local weather service alerts — record in the Daily WBGT Log.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Proactively adjust schedules and enforce strict work-rest controls when WBGT exceeds 28–30 °C.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Intervene immediately if any symptoms appear and ensure prompt medical evaluation.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Escalate to Site Manager if a heat wave is forecast or WBGT exceeds 32 °C.</span></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.3 Provision of Water</h3>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Potable, cool drinking water available at all times — minimum 3–5 litres per person per day, scaling to 1 litre/hour during peak heat.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Hydration stations at the rig site, rest areas and inside all vehicles.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Encourage small, frequent sips rather than infrequent heavy gulping.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Electrolyte replacements (ORS sachets) available for heavy sweat loss and Ramadan fasting recovery.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Water temperature: cool but not cold — 10–15 °C preferred.</span></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.4 Access to Shade and Rest Areas</h3>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Provide purpose-built shaded rest areas (gazebos, industrial tents or air-conditioned containers) with open air flow.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Schedule mandatory breaks in shade — e.g., 10–15 minutes every hour when WBGT is high.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Vehicles and trucks may be used as shade only if air-conditioned and not parked in direct, radiating sun.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Rest areas equipped with cold water, electrolytes, first-aid supplies and ambient thermometer.</span></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.6 Heat-Related Illnesses — Signs, Symptoms and Treatment</h3>

<div class="overflow-x-auto mb-4 rounded border" style="border-color: #dde3ec;">
  <table class="w-full text-sm border-collapse">
    <thead>
      <tr style="background-color: #081C2E;">
        <th class="text-left px-3 py-2 font-semibold tracking-wide text-xs" style="color: #ffffff;">Condition</th>
        <th class="text-left px-3 py-2 font-semibold tracking-wide text-xs" style="color: #ffffff;">Signs and Symptoms</th>

      <tr style="background-color: rgba(8,28,46,0.03);"><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs font-medium" style="color: #081C2E; width: 22%;">Heat Rash (Prickly Heat)</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151; width: 39%;">Red blotches, itchiness in damp/sweaty areas; prickling sensation.</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151;">Move to cool environment; cool shower; dry skin thoroughly; wear loose clothing.</td></tr>
      <tr><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs font-medium" style="color: #081C2E; width: 22%;">Heat Cramps</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151; width: 39%;">Painful involuntary muscle spasms, typically legs or abdomen.</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151;">Rest in shade; hydrate with water and electrolytes; gentle stretching; return to work only after full recovery.</td></tr>

      <tr style="background-color: rgba(8,28,46,0.03);"><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs font-medium" style="color: #081C2E; width: 22%;">Heat Stroke (EMERGENCY)</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151; width: 39%;">Body temp &gt;40 °C; confusion, disorientation, seizures; NO sweating (hot/dry skin); unconsciousness.</td><td class="px-3 py-2 text-xs" style="color: #374151;">Call 997 immediately. Move to shade; initiate rapid cooling (ice packs to neck/armpits/groin, wet sheets, fans); do NOT give fluids if unconscious; transport to hospital.</td></tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.7 Control Measures</h3>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Acclimatisation — gradual heat exposure over 5–14 days for new or returning workers — start at 20% workload and increase progressively.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Buddy System — workers pair up to actively monitor each other for physical symptoms during all hot-weather work.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Scheduling — schedule the heaviest manual work during cooler early morning (05:00–10:00) or late afternoon (16:00–19:00) hours.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Cooling Gear — cooling vests, neck shades, industrial misting fans and ice-pack belts where practical.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Pre-Shift Health Check — visual fitness check before assignment to heavy outdoor work; confirm hydration and rest.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Culture — strict non-punitive reporting for any heat-related symptoms; Stop Work Authority applies.</span></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.8 Incident Reporting and Review</h3>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Report any heat-related illness (from severe cramps to heat stroke) immediately using the Incident Flash Notification Form (TE-IMS-FRM-HSE-002).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Investigate root cause per the Continual Improvement and CAPA Procedure (TE-IMS-PROC-SYS-005).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Review site controls and update work-rest cycles based on lessons learned.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Notify MHRSD where reporting thresholds are met for occupational illness.</span></li>
</ul>
