<h2 id="s1" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">1. Policy Statement</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;">At True East Mining Company (TEMC) no task is so important that it cannot be done safely. Every employee, contractor, and visitor has the absolute authority — and the duty — to stop work immediately whenever a condition, act, or situation presents an imminent risk of injury, environmental harm, or property damage. Stop Work Authority (SWA) is exercised without fear of reprisal.</p>

<h2 id="s2" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">2. Key Principles</h2>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span><strong>Right and Duty to Stop Work</strong> — any person who observes an unsafe condition or act must stop the work immediately. No prior permission from a supervisor is required.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span><strong>No Retaliation</strong> — TEMC guarantees zero retaliation against any individual exercising SWA in good faith. Intimidation of anyone stopping work is itself a disciplinary offence.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span><strong>Good Faith</strong> — SWA is a safety tool, not a productivity tool. It must be exercised honestly and in good faith.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span><strong>Safe Restart Protocol</strong> — work may only resume once the hazard is controlled, the Job Hazard Analysis is updated, and all parties agree it is safe. High-risk tasks (confined space, hot work, working at height, lifting) require HSE or Site Manager approval to restart.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s3" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">3. Examples of When to Stop Work</h2>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1">
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Unsafe scaffolding or missing fall protection.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Equipment malfunction or missing machine guards.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Gas detector alarms or hazardous atmospheres.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Visible symptoms of fatigue or heat stress.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Uncontrolled spills or fire risks.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Missing or invalid permit to work.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>LOTO or isolation not in place.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Unqualified personnel performing a high-risk task.</span></li>
</ul>

<p class="text-sm font-bold mt-3" style="color: #081C2E;">If in doubt — STOP.</p>

<h2 id="s4" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">4. Process After a Stop Work Event</h2>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1">
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>The person stopping work notifies the Supervisor and HSE immediately.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>The area is secured and personnel moved to a safe position.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>A Flash Notification (FRM-HSE-002) is raised to capture the event.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>A Job Hazard Analysis (or Take-5) is completed or updated before restart.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>HSE or the Site Manager verifies controls before approving restart (where high-risk).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>The event is reviewed for learning and shared through toolbox talks.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s5" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">5. Training and Awareness</h2>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1">
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Mandatory SWA training is delivered at induction and annually.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Training records are maintained in the Training and Competence Matrix (REG-TRN-001).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>SWA principles are reinforced in toolbox talks and posted at every site.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s6" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">6. Monitoring</h2>

<ul class="list-none pl-0 space-y-1">
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>All SWA events are logged and trended by the HSE Manager.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>SWA performance is reported at the Management Review (PROC-SYS-002) as a leading indicator of safety culture.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 text-sm" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28;">•</span><span>Suppressed or ignored SWA events are treated as Major non-conformances under PROC-SYS-008.</span></li>
</ul>
