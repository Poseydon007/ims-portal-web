<h2 id="s1" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">1. Purpose</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">The purpose of this procedure is to eliminate the causes of fire and prevent loss of life and property at all True East Mining Company (TEMC) facilities and worksites. It provides employees, contractors, and visitors with information and guidelines that will assist them in recognising, reporting, and controlling fire hazards, and responding effectively to fire emergencies. This procedure supports ISO 45001:2018 (Clauses 6.1.2, 8.1.2, 8.2 Emergency Preparedness) and aligns with Saudi Civil Defense and MHRSD requirements.</p>

<h2 id="s2" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">2. Scope</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">This procedure applies to all TEMC employees, contractors, visitors, facilities, and properties — including offices, camps, workshops, fuel storage areas, core yards, and drill sites.</p>

<h2 id="s4" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">4. Identification of Fire Hazards</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Fire and explosion hazards can exist in almost any work area. Potential hazards include:</p>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Improper operation or maintenance of gas-fired equipment.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Improper storage or use of flammable liquids (diesel, petrol, solvents, paints).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Smoking in prohibited areas.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Accumulation of trash, oily rags, and combustible waste.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Unauthorised hot work operations (welding, grinding, cutting).</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Electrical faults, overloaded circuits, damaged cables.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Hot surfaces near flammable materials.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s5" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">5. Control and Prevention</h2>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">5.1 Elimination of Ignition and Explosion Sources</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">All non-essential ignition and explosion sources must be eliminated where flammable liquids are used or stored. Common potential sources include:</p>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Open flames — cutting and welding torches, furnaces, matches, and heaters kept away from flammable liquids. Cutting or welding on flammable liquid equipment is not performed unless the equipment has been properly emptied and purged with a neutral gas such as nitrogen, under permit.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Electrical sources — DC motors, switches, and circuit breakers are eliminated where flammable liquids are handled or stored. Only approved explosion-proof devices are used in these areas.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Static sparks — can generate from electron transfer between contacting surfaces. Proper bonding and grounding procedures are followed when flammable liquids are transferred or transported.</span></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">5.2 Removal of Incompatibles</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Materials that can contribute to a flammable liquid fire (e.g. oxidisers and organic peroxides) are not stored with flammable liquids.</p>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">5.3 Smoking</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Smoking and other sources of ignition are prohibited near flammable or explosive materials or operations that constitute a fire hazard. "NO SMOKING OR OPEN FLAMES" signs are posted conspicuously in all such areas.</p>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">5.4 Control of Flammable Gases</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Flammable gases pose the same type of fire hazards as flammable liquids and their vapours. Safeguards for flammable liquids apply, but toxicity, reactivity, corrosiveness, and toxic combustion products are also taken into account.</p>

<h2 id="s6" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">6. Fire Extinguishers</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">A portable fire extinguisher is a "first aid" device highly effective when used while a fire is small. Using the correct class of extinguisher by a trained person can save lives and property. Extinguishers are installed regardless of other firefighting measures.</p>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.1 Classification of Fires and Selection of Extinguishers</h3>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Class A — fires involving materials such as wood, paper, and cloth which produce glowing embers or char.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Class B — fires involving flammable gases, liquids, and greases, including gasoline and most hydrocarbon liquids.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Class C — fires involving live electrical equipment or materials near electrically powered equipment.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Class D — fires involving combustible metals, such as magnesium, zirconium, potassium, and sodium.</span></li>
</ul>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Extinguishers are selected according to the potential fire hazard, construction and occupancy of facilities, and the specific hazard to be protected.</p>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.2 Location and Marking of Extinguishers</h3>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Conspicuously located and readily accessible along normal paths of travel and egress.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Wall recesses or flush-mounted cabinets used where possible.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Directional arrows marked with the extinguisher classification where visual obstruction cannot be avoided.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Grouped extinguishers of different classes marked conspicuously (legible from 3 feet) to ensure proper selection during a fire.</span></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.3 Condition and Maintenance</h3>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Portable extinguishers are maintained in a fully charged and operable condition, kept in designated locations when not in use, and replaced with a fully charged and operable unit whenever removed for maintenance. Monthly visual inspections and annual servicing are recorded on the Fire Extinguisher Inspection Log (FRM-HSE-020).</p>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.4 Mounting and Distribution</h3>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Installed on hangers, brackets, shelves, or in cabinets.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Units weighing 40 lb (18 kg) or less are installed so the top is no more than 3.5 ft (1.07 m) above the floor.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Operating instructions face outward. Cabinets and recesses are marked in contrasting colours to the normal décor.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Travel distance limits: Class A and D — 75 ft (23 m) maximum; Class B — 50 ft (15 m) maximum. Class C distribution follows patterns appropriate for Class A and B hazards.</span></li>
</ul>

<h3 class="text-sm font-bold mt-5 mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6.5 PASS Method of Use</h3>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Pull the pin to break the tamper seal.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Aim the nozzle at the base of the fire.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Squeeze the handle to release the extinguishing agent.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Sweep the nozzle from side to side until the fire is completely out.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s7" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">7. Fire Safety Inspections and Housekeeping</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">First-line supervisors and the Safety Inspector conduct worksite surveys to observe compliance, proper storage of chemicals and supplies, unobstructed extinguisher access, and clear emergency evacuation routes. Findings are recorded on the Fire Prevention Checklist (FRM-HSE-021).</p>

<h2 id="s8" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">8. Emergency Exits</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">Every exit must be clearly visible or the route to it conspicuously identified. AT NO TIME SHALL EXITS BE BLOCKED. Exits are checked daily by site supervision.</p>

<h2 id="s9" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">9. Emergencies Involving Fire</h2>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>9.1 Fire Alarms — a fire alarm sounds for the building or surroundings in an emergency.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>9.2 Evacuation Plan — each facility has an emergency evacuation plan developed by the Safety Officer leading to a designated Assembly Point. The colour-coded plan includes evacuation routes, emergency exits, fire extinguisher locations, heat and smoke detectors, fire alarm switches, fire protection equipment, and the Assembly Point.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>9.3 Elevators — the use of elevators during a fire is strictly prohibited due to the risk of power cuts and the shaft acting as a flue for hot gases and smoke.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>9.4 Accountability — Department Heads, the Safety Officer, and Supervisors are responsible for verifying personnel have evacuated assigned areas and for conducting a headcount at the Assembly Point.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s10" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">10. Firefighting and Emergency Response</h2>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">If you discover a fire:</p>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Activate the nearest fire alarm.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Notify other occupants and the Department Head / Safety Inspector / Supervisor.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Call Saudi Civil Defense (998) for any fire beyond incipient stage.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Fight the fire ONLY if: you have a clear way out and can fight the fire with your back to the exit; the fire is small and limited to its area of origin; the fire department has been notified; you have the proper extinguisher in good working order and know how to use it.</span></li>
</ul>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">If you are not sure of your ability or the extinguisher's capacity, leave the area immediately.</p>

<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #081C2E;">If you hear a fire alarm:</p>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Evacuate the area. Close windows, turn off gas jets, and close doors as you leave.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Leave the building and move out of the way of emergency operations.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Proceed to the designated Assembly Point.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Report to the Safety Inspector to confirm your evacuation.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Remain outside until a competent authority states it is safe to re-enter.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s11" class="text-sm font-bold mt-8 mb-3 pb-2" style="color: #081C2E; border-bottom: 2px solid #C49A28;">11. Evacuation Routes</h2>

<ul class="space-y-1 mb-4 list-none pl-0">
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Learn at least two escape routes and emergency exits from your area.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Never use an elevator as part of your escape route.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Learn to activate a fire alarm and recognise alarm sounds.</span></li>
  <li class="flex gap-2 mb-2 text-sm leading-relaxed" style="color: #374151;"><span style="color: #C49A28; margin-top: 2px;">•</span><span>Take an active part in fire evacuation drills (recorded on FRM-HSE-018 Emergency Evacuation Drill Record and FRM-HSE-019 Fire Drill Report).</span></li>
</ul>
