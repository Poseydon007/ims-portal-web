<h2 id="s1" class="text-base font-bold mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">1. Purpose</h2>
<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-6" style="color: #374151;">This Plan establishes a proactive framework for preventing falls from height and ensuring the rapid, effective rescue of personnel suspended or injured during work at height at any True East Mining Company (TEMC) operation in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. It is aligned with ISO 45001:2018, MHRSD occupational safety regulations, Saudi Civil Defense rescue standards, and recognised international fall-protection standards.</p>

<h2 id="s2" class="text-base font-bold mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">2. Scope</h2>
<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-6" style="color: #374151;">This Plan applies to all TEMC employees, contractors, and visitors performing or supervising work at height — defined as 1.8 metres (6 feet) or more above a lower level — at drilling rigs, derricks, masts, workshops, fuel tanks, crushers, stockpiles, tall structures, ladders, scaffolds, MEWPs, and any temporary work platform. It applies to both routine and unplanned rescue events.</p>

<h2 id="s3" class="text-base font-bold mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">3. Definitions</h2>
<ul class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-6 space-y-1.5" style="color: #374151;">
  <li>• <strong>Work at Height</strong> — any task where a person could fall a distance liable to cause injury — ≥1.8 m in TEMC practice.</li>
  <li>• <strong>Fall Arrest System</strong> — a full-body harness connected to a shock-absorbing lanyard or self-retracting lifeline (SRL) anchored to a certified anchor point.</li>
  <li>• <strong>Rescue Plan</strong> — a site- or task-specific written plan detailing the rescue method, team, equipment, and timing for a specific work-at-height activity.</li>
  <li>• <strong>Suspension Trauma</strong> — orthostatic intolerance resulting from prolonged immobile suspension in a harness — potentially fatal within 15 minutes.</li>
  <li>• <strong>Competent Person</strong> — a person with specific training and experience to perform rescue, inspect equipment, or approve a Rescue Plan.</li>
  <li>• <strong>Certified Anchor</strong> — anchor point rated ≥ 22.2 kN (5000 lbs) per ANSI/ASSP Z359 or equivalent, inspected and approved before use.</li>
  <li>• <strong>Self-Rescue</strong> — the worker descends or returns to a safe level unaided, using their own fall protection system.</li>
  <li>• <strong>Assisted Rescue</strong> — the rescue team uses ropes, ladders, pulleys, or MEWPs to raise or lower the casualty.</li>
  <li>• <strong>Mechanical Rescue</strong> — use of cranes, hoists, or aerial platforms for high-angle or heavy-load rescue.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s5" class="text-base font-bold mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">5. Pre-Task Risk Assessment (ISO 45001 Clause 6.1.2)</h2>
<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #374151;">Before any work at height commences, a specific Job Safety Analysis (JSA) and Permit to Work (PTW) must be completed and signed.</p>
<ul class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-6 space-y-1.5" style="color: #374151;">
  <li>• Identify hazards — falls, falling objects, unstable surfaces, wind, heat stress in the KSA desert environment, dust storms, electrical hazards, proximity to moving machinery.</li>
  <li>• Assess rescue constraints — time required to reach the worker, rescue equipment needs, medical support distance, communication reliability.</li>
  <li>• Weather check — stop work when wind exceeds 40 km/h, during sandstorms, lightning, or when heat index triggers the TEMC heat stress procedure.</li>
  <li>• Output — a task-specific Rescue Plan, integrated with the JSA and PTW, approved by HSE before work begins.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s6" class="text-base font-bold mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">6. Rescue Plan Development</h2>
<p class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-3" style="color: #374151;">Every work-at-height task requires a written Rescue Plan covering:</p>
<ul class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-6 space-y-1.5" style="color: #374151;">
  <li>• Designated rescue team — minimum 2 trained rescuers, on standby at the work location.</li>
  <li>• Communication — dedicated channel, emergency signal, secondary method if primary fails.</li>
  <li>• Hierarchy of rescue methods — Self-Rescue first, then Assisted, then Mechanical.</li>
  <li>• Equipment list — verified present, inspected, and pre-staged.</li>
  <li>• Medical support — first aiders, AED, ambulance standby or contact, nearest hospital identified.</li>
  <li>• Suspension trauma mitigation — target rescue time ≤ 10 minutes, absolute maximum 15 minutes. Trauma relief straps on every harness. Conscious casualties instructed to pump legs.</li>
  <li>• Stop Work conditions — wind, lightning, dust, equipment failure, crew incapacity.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s8" class="text-base font-bold mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">8. Equipment and Preparation</h2>
<ul class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-6 space-y-1.5" style="color: #374151;">
  <li>• Fall Protection — full-body harness, shock-absorbing lanyard, self-retracting lifeline (SRL), certified anchors ≥22.2 kN (5000 lbs).</li>
  <li>• Rescue Equipment — ropes and pulleys (3:1 or 4:1 mechanical advantage), rescue ladders, telescopic reach poles, rescue stretchers or baskets, trauma relief straps, AED.</li>
  <li>• Inspection — daily pre-use visual by user; formal documented weekly/monthly inspection by competent person. Discard and tag-out any item failing inspection.</li>
  <li>• Staging — rescue equipment pre-positioned at or within 10 m of the work area for rapid access.</li>
  <li>• Lone Working — not permitted at height where rescue may be required — minimum 2 workers plus rescue standby.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s9" class="text-base font-bold mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">9. Rescue Execution (Event of Fall / Suspension)</h2>
<ul class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-6 space-y-1.5" style="color: #374151;">
  <li>• Immediate response — raise alarm via radio or emergency signal. Secure area below the suspended worker. Barricade against falling objects.</li>
  <li>{"• Assessment — assess worker's condition (conscious, injured, unresponsive) and environmental stability (weather, structure integrity)."}</li>
  <li>• Call for support — HSE Manager, site medics, Civil Defense 998 if rescue may exceed 10 minutes or casualty is injured.</li>
  <li>• Execute — rescuers don PPE and attach to independent certified anchors. Access the worker via the chosen method. Always prefer non-entry remote rescue.</li>
  <li>• Stabilise and transfer — attach the casualty to the rescue system and raise or lower to a secure level.</li>
  <li>• Post-rescue care — provide first aid, treat suspension trauma — position the casualty in a recovery position per current medical guidance, NOT strictly horizontal; monitor airway, breathing, and circulation. Arrange transfer to hospital.</li>
  <li>• Time target — on-site rescue within 10 minutes of the event; critical maximum 15 minutes.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s10" class="text-base font-bold mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">10. Suspension Trauma — Critical Notes</h2>
<ul class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-6 space-y-1.5" style="color: #374151;">
  <li>• Suspension trauma can cause loss of consciousness in as little as 5 minutes in an inverted or motionless person.</li>
  <li>• Casualty should pump their legs, use trauma relief straps, or rest feet on any available surface if conscious.</li>
  <li>• After rescue, monitor for delayed reaction (reflow syndrome). Do NOT lay a long-suspended casualty fully flat immediately — use a sitting or slight incline position while monitoring airway and vital signs, per current international guidance.</li>
  <li>• Transfer to medical care as soon as possible, even if the casualty appears uninjured.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s11" class="text-base font-bold mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">11. Training and Drills</h2>
<ul class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-6 space-y-1.5" style="color: #374151;">
  <li>• All personnel receive fall protection training before any work at height — harness use, anchor selection, inspection, rescue awareness.</li>
  <li>• Rescue Team members hold advanced certification — high-angle rescue, first aid / CPR, suspension trauma awareness.</li>
  <li>• Drills conducted quarterly using realistic scenarios — suspended worker, injured worker, night operations, dust storm delay.</li>
  <li>• Participation, timing, and system effectiveness are documented and debriefed for continual improvement.</li>
  <li>• Refresher training annual or immediately following any incident or identified gap.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s12" class="text-base font-bold mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">12. Incident Reporting, Investigation, and Review</h2>
<ul class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-6 space-y-1.5" style="color: #374151;">
  <li>• Every fall, suspension, near-miss, and rescue event is reported immediately through the TEMC incident reporting system.</li>
  <li>• Root cause analysis per PROC-HSE-006 and ISO 45001 Clause 10.2.</li>
  <li>• Rescue plans and procedures are updated post-drill, post-incident, or following regulatory changes.</li>
  <li>• Lessons learned shared across all TEMC sites via safety bulletins and toolbox talks.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="s13" class="text-base font-bold mb-2" style="color: #081C2E;">13. Escalation to Site Emergency Response</h2>
<ul class="text-sm leading-relaxed mb-6 space-y-1.5" style="color: #374151;">
  <li>• Activate the full Major Emergency Preparedness Plan (PLN-HSE-001) if the rescue escalates beyond the local team — structural collapse, multiple casualties, fire, or gas release.</li>
  <li>• Coordinate urgent medical evacuation with Saudi Red Crescent (997) and the nearest hospital.</li>
  <li>• OSCEC takes command in escalated events.</li>
</ul>
