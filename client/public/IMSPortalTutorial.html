What Is the IMS Document Portal?

The IMS Document Portal is the central digital system for True East Mining Company's Integrated Management System (IMS). It is the single place where all company documents, forms, procedures, policies, and records are stored, accessed, submitted, and controlled.

Before this portal existed, documents were scattered across email threads, shared drives, USB sticks, and printed folders. Version control was unreliable, approvals happened informally, and there was no audit trail. The portal solves all of that.

ℹ️
IMS stands for Integrated Management System. It is the framework that combines quality (ISO 9001), health & safety (ISO 45001), and environment (ISO 14001) management into a single unified system. True East's IMS is aligned with all three international standards plus KSA Mining Investment Law and MHRSD OHS Regulations.

What the Portal Does

The portal has five core functions:

Function What It Means in Practice
Document Library Browse and read all controlled IMS documents — policies, procedures, SOPs, forms, registers, plans, and reference materials. Always the current approved version.
Form Submission Submit digital forms (Near Miss reports, JHAs, Permits to Work, inspection checklists, etc.) directly from any device. No paper, no printing, no scanning.
Approval Workflow Every form submission goes through a 3-step approval chain (Supervisor → HSE Manager → Final Approval). The portal tracks every step, sends email notifications, and maintains a full audit trail.
Occurrence Log All submissions are automatically recorded in a cumulative register. Admins can download the full log as an Excel file at any time.
Education & Training Access training materials, manuals, and guides. Completion of training quizzes is tracked per user.

Who Uses It?

Every employee at True East Mining Company uses the portal. The level of access depends on your role. A field worker can submit forms and read documents. A supervisor can approve submissions from their team. An HSE Manager can review and approve at a higher level. An admin has full control over everything.

✅
The portal is accessible from any device with a browser — desktop, laptop, tablet, or mobile phone. No app installation required. You only need your company email and password.

Reading and Finding Documents

The document library is the core of the portal. Every controlled document in the True East IMS is stored here. Documents are organized by category and identified by a unique code.

Understanding Document Codes

Every document has a code that tells you exactly what it is and where it belongs. The format is:

TE-IMS-POL-GOV-001
Company    System    Type       Department   Sequence

Example: TE-IMS-FRM-HSE-003 means: True East / IMS / Form / Health, Safety & Environment / number 003 — which is the Near Miss Report Form.

How to Find a Document

1
From the Home page, click the relevant category card

For example, if you want to find a policy, click the "Policies" card. If you want a form, click "Forms".

2
Browse the document list

The category page shows all documents in that category as a table. Each row shows the document code, title, revision number, date, and status.

3
Click "View" to open the document

Documents open as formatted HTML pages directly in the browser. No PDF download required. The document is always the current approved version.

4
For forms with a submission button, click "Fill Form"

Forms that can be submitted digitally have a "Fill Form" button that opens the interactive form. See Section 5 for how to submit forms.

Document Status Indicators

Status Meaning
Approved The document is current, reviewed, and approved. This is the version you should use.
Pending The document exists in the portal but is still under review or awaiting final approval. Use with caution — confirm with your supervisor.
Draft The document is in preparation. Not yet for operational use.
⚠️
Document Control Notice: All documents in this portal are controlled under the True East Mining Company IMS. Printed copies are uncontrolled. Always refer to this portal for the current approved version. For document requests or submissions, contact the Management Representative.

Submitting Forms

Forms are the most frequently used part of the portal for field staff. Instead of filling in paper forms and handing them to a supervisor, you fill in the digital form on any device and submit it. The system automatically routes it through the approval chain, sends email notifications, and records everything in the occurrence log.

How to Find and Open a Form

From the Home page, click the "Forms" (FRM) category card. You will see a list of all available forms. Find the form you need and click "Fill Form".

💡
Direct URL shortcut: If you know the form code, you can go directly to it. For example, the Near Miss Report Form is at /forms/frm-hse-003. Your supervisor may share direct links for the forms your team uses most often.

Filling In a Form — Step by Step

Let's use the Near Miss Report Form (TE-IMS-FRM-HSE-003) as a concrete example. The same process applies to all forms.

1
Auto-filled fields appear at the top

Your Full Name, Employee ID, Position, Department, and Submission Date are automatically filled in from your account. These fields are locked — you cannot change them. This ensures accurate attribution of every report.

2
Fill in the Report Details section

Enter the Date of Occurrence, Time of Occurrence, Location/Site Area, and Site/Project Name. The Report Number (e.g., NM-2026-001) is generated automatically — you do not need to enter it.

3
Complete each section in order

The form is divided into logical sections. For the Near Miss form: (1) Report Details, (2) Classification, (3) Description, (4) Contributing Factors, (5) Risk Rating, (6) Corrective Actions, (7) Sign-off. Work through each section completely before moving to the next.

4
Complete the Risk Rating section

Select the Likelihood (1–5) and Consequence (1–4) of the near miss. The Risk Rating (Low / Medium / High / Extreme) is calculated automatically from your selections. You do not need to calculate it yourself.

5
Enter Corrective Actions

For each corrective action, enter: what the action is, who is responsible, and the target due date. You can add multiple corrective actions using the "Add Row" button.

6
Review and submit

Read through the form to check your entries. Required fields are marked with a red asterisk (*). If any required field is empty, the form will not submit and will highlight what is missing. When everything is complete, click "Submit Report".

What Happens After You Submit

Immediately after submission, the following happens automatically:

  1. A unique Report Number is assigned (e.g., NM-2026-001)
  2. The form status is set to Pending Supervisor Review
  3. Your direct supervisor receives an email notification with the report number and a link to review it
  4. The submission is recorded in the Near Miss Occurrence Log
  5. You receive a confirmation email with the report number
✅
You can track your submission by going to the Home page and clicking on the "My Submissions" section (if visible for your role), or by asking your supervisor for the status. Admins can see all submissions in the Submissions panel.

The Approval Workflow

Every form submission goes through a structured 3-step approval process before it is closed. This ensures that every report is reviewed by the right people at the right level, and that nothing falls through the cracks.

The Three Steps

Step 1
Supervisor
→
Step 2
HSE Manager
→
Step 3
Final Approval
→
Closed ✓
Step Who Acts Status During This Step What They Do
Step 1 Direct Supervisor Pending Supervisor Review Reviews the submission, verifies the facts, checks the corrective actions, and either approves or returns with comments.
Step 2 HSE Manager Pending HSE Manager Review Reviews from an HSE perspective, assesses the risk rating, verifies corrective actions are adequate, and either approves or returns.
Step 3 Admin / Management Pending Final Approval Final sign-off. Confirms the report is complete and the corrective actions are formally accepted. Closes the report.
Closed — Closed The report is fully processed and archived. It remains in the occurrence log permanently.

How to Approve a Submission (Supervisors and HSE Managers)

1
You receive an email notification

When a submission reaches your step, you receive an email with the report number, the submitter's name, and a direct link to review it.

2
Click the link or go to Approvals in the portal

The "Approvals" tab in the top navigation shows all submissions currently waiting for your action. The count badge shows how many are pending.

3
Click "Review" to open the submission

You will see the full form as submitted, with all fields and the submitter's information. Review every section carefully.

4
Choose to Approve or Return

If the submission is complete and correct, click "Approve". The form moves to the next step automatically. If something is wrong or missing, click "Return" — you must enter a comment explaining what needs to be corrected.

What Happens When a Form is Returned

If a submission is returned at any step, the following happens:

  1. The status changes to Returned
  2. The original submitter receives an email with the return reason
  3. The submission goes back to Step 1 (Supervisor Review) — it restarts the full workflow
  4. The submitter can view the return comment in the submission detail
  5. The submitter should correct the issue and resubmit
ℹ️
All approval actions are permanently recorded. The audit trail shows who approved or returned each submission, at what time, and what comment they left. This cannot be deleted or edited after the fact.

User Roles and Permissions

The portal has four user roles. Your role determines what you can see and do. Roles are assigned by the system administrator and reflect your position in the company.

🔧 Field Worker

  • Sign in and view documents
  • Submit forms
  • View own submissions
  • Access Education section
  • Cannot approve submissions

👷 Supervisor

  • All Field Worker permissions
  • Approve / Return submissions at Step 1
  • View submissions from their team
  • Receive Step 1 email notifications

🦺 HSE Manager

  • All Supervisor permissions
  • Approve / Return at Step 2
  • View all HSE submissions
  • Receive Step 2 email notifications

⭐ Admin

  • All permissions
  • Final approval at Step 3
  • View all submissions (all forms)
  • Edit report numbers
  • Delete submissions
  • Manage users
  • Download Excel logs
⚠️
Your role is set by the administrator. If you believe your role is incorrect (for example, you are a supervisor but cannot see the Approvals tab), contact the system administrator to verify and update your account.

Admin Functions

Admins have access to three additional sections: Submissions, Users, and the full Near Miss Occurrence Log. These are only visible to users with the Admin role.

All Submissions (Admin View)

The Submissions page shows every form submission across all users and all form types. It has two sections:

Section 1 — Submissions Table

A filterable table showing all submissions. You can filter by status: All Pending Supervisor Pending HSE Manager Pending Final Approval Returned Closed

For each submission, admins can:

  • View — open the full submission in read-only mode
  • Edit No. — manually correct the report number if needed
  • Delete — permanently remove the submission (use with caution)

Section 2 — Near Miss Occurrence Log

Below the submissions table is a horizontal spreadsheet-style register showing all Near Miss submissions with every form field as a column. This is the cumulative occurrence log. It includes:

  • Report No., Submission Date, Submitter Name, Employee ID, Department, Site
  • Date and Time of Occurrence, Location
  • Classification, Description, Immediate Action
  • Contributing Factors, Risk Likelihood, Risk Consequence, Risk Rating
  • Corrective Actions, Status

Click the green "↓ Download Excel" button to export the full log as an .xlsx file. The export includes all submissions regardless of the current filter.

User Management

The Users page allows admins to manage all portal accounts. From this page you can:

  • Create a new user — enter their full name, email, employee ID, department, position, and assign a role. The system generates login credentials.
  • Edit a user — update any field including role and department.
  • Activate / Deactivate — deactivated users cannot sign in but their data is preserved.
  • Reset password — generate a new temporary password for a user who is locked out.
💡
Best practice: When an employee leaves the company, deactivate their account immediately — do not delete it. Deletion removes the audit trail for all their submissions. Deactivation preserves the record while preventing further access.

Frequently Asked Questions

I forgot my password. What do I do?

Contact your system administrator or the Management Representative. They will reset your password and provide you with a new temporary one. There is no self-service password reset — all account changes go through the administrator.

I submitted a form but made a mistake. Can I edit it?

Once submitted, you cannot edit a form yourself. However, the approver at Step 1 (your supervisor) can return the form to you with a comment explaining what needs to be corrected. Once returned, the form goes back to you and you can resubmit with the corrections. If the form has already been approved and closed, contact the admin to discuss options.

Why is my name and employee ID already filled in on the form?

The portal automatically fills in your identity fields (Full Name, Employee ID, Position, Department, Submission Date) from your account profile. This ensures accurate attribution and prevents errors. These fields are locked and cannot be changed on the form — if your profile information is wrong, contact the administrator to update your account.

I don't see the Approvals tab. Why?

The Approvals tab is only visible to users with the Supervisor, HSE Manager, or Admin role. If you are a supervisor but cannot see it, your role in the system may not have been set correctly. Contact the administrator to verify your role.

What is the difference between "Pending HSE Manager" and "Pending Final Approval"?

These are two separate steps in the approval chain. "Pending HSE Manager" means the form is at Step 2 — waiting for the HSE Manager to review and approve. "Pending Final Approval" means it has passed the HSE Manager and is at Step 3 — waiting for the Admin/Management to give final sign-off and close the report.

How do I know if my submission has been approved?

You receive an email notification at each step. When the form is fully closed, you receive a final email confirming closure. You can also ask your supervisor or the admin to check the status.

Can I access the portal on my phone?

Yes. The portal is fully responsive and works on mobile browsers. Open your phone's browser, navigate to the portal URL, and sign in normally. All features — including form submission — work on mobile.

What is the DTD system and why do I need to learn it?

DTD stands for Day-to-Day Document. It is the numbering and registration system for all operational documents you produce in your daily work — purchase orders, letters, reports, memos, etc. Every document you create should be registered with a DTD code so it can be tracked and retrieved. The DTD Training Manual in the Education section covers this in full detail.

I see a document marked "Pending" — can I use it?

A "Pending" document exists in the portal but has not yet received final approval. You should use it with caution and confirm with your supervisor or the Management Representative before relying on it for operational decisions. Always prefer "Approved" documents where available.

Who do I contact for portal support?

For account issues (login, password, role), contact the system administrator.
For document questions (content, revision, approval), contact the Management Representative.
For technical issues (the portal is not loading, a form is not working), contact the administrator and describe the issue with as much detail as possible (what page, what action, what error message).
