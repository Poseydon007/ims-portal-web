Chapter One

Why PPE matters.

On a drilling site in the KSA desert, hazards do not wait. Falling objects, grinding sparks, 48°C heat, silica dust, noise above 100 dB — these are daily realities. Your PPE stands between you and the injury. Wear it wrong once and the protection disappears.

1

It absorbs the impact

When objects fall, equipment fails, or chemicals splash — PPE takes the hit so your body does not. A hard hat can survive a 4 kg object at 2 metres. Your skull cannot.

2

It keeps you compliant

Saudi MHRSD regulations and ISO 45001 require adequate PPE for all hazardous tasks. Failure means a stop-work order, regulatory fines, and personal liability for both worker and supervisor.

3

It protects long-term health

Dust and noise injuries build silently. Silicosis from unprotected rock dust can be fatal years later. Noise-induced hearing loss is permanent. Today's discomfort beats a lifetime of damage.

“ No task is urgent enough to skip PPE. If you don’t have the right gear, you don’t start the task.

PPE Category 01

Hard Hat

Protects against falling objects, bump impacts, and electrical contact. Class A or E for electrical work. Required on all TEMC sites at all times outside of rest areas.

Inspect Before Use
  1. Check shell for cracks, dents, or holes
  2. Check suspension harness — no broken straps or fraying
  3. Confirm no paint, stickers, or drill holes on shell (these weaken it)
  4. Check for UV degradation — chalky or faded surface means replace
  5. Verify manufacture date stamp — replace shell after 3 years
Correct Fit
  • Adjust headband — 2 finger gap between shell and forehead
  • Brim faces forward (unless task requires reverse — check with supervisor)
  • Use chinstrap at height, near overhead lifts, or in wind
  • Never wear a cap or beanie underneath — changes fit and protection
  • In summer: fit approved neck-flap or sun-shade attachment
Storage
  • Store away from direct sunlight and extreme heat
  • Keep off the rear window shelf of vehicles (UV + impact risk)
  • Never leave on rig floor unprotected from falling tools
  • Clean with mild soap — no solvents or harsh chemicals
  • Store suspension separate from shell if packing for transit
PPE Category 02

Safety Glasses & Goggles

Safety glasses protect against flying particles, dust, and debris. Chemical splash goggles create a sealed barrier against liquids. Choose the right type for the task — they are not interchangeable.

Inspect Before Use
  1. Check lenses for scratches, cracks, or pitting
  2. Check frame for warping or broken temples
  3. For goggles: test strap elasticity — stretched straps fail seal
  4. For welding lenses: verify correct shade number for the process
  5. Scratched lenses reduce optical clarity — replace immediately
Correct Fit
  • Safety glasses: side shields must cover peripheral area
  • Goggles: seal firmly to face with no gaps at nose bridge
  • Prescription users: over-glasses (OTG) type or prescription insert
  • Welding: safety glasses worn under the welding shield at all times
  • Anti-fog lenses recommended for KSA heat and cold mornings
Storage
  • Store in protective pouch or hard case when not in use
  • Never face-down on hard surfaces
  • Keep away from chemicals, solvents, and extreme heat
  • Clean with lens wipes — not clothing fabric (causes micro-scratches)
  • Label goggles with your name if shared units are not used
PPE Category 03

Hearing Protection

Drilling rigs and generators frequently exceed 100 dB — well above the KSA action level of 85 dB. Noise-induced hearing loss is irreversible. Use earplugs or earmuffs in all designated noise zones.

Inspect Before Use
  1. Earplugs: check for dirt, hardening, or deformation
  2. Earmuffs: check cushion seals for cracks or flattening
  3. Earmuffs: check headband tension — replace if loose
  4. Disposable foam earplugs: use once only, then discard
  5. Check SNR/NRR rating label is still legible on earmuffs
Correct Fit
  • Foam plugs: roll thin, pull ear up-and-back, insert, hold 20 seconds
  • Earmuffs: cushions must fully seal over both ears — check with glasses
  • No gaps allowed — a 1 mm gap reduces attenuation by 15 dB
  • Combined use (plugs + muffs) for very high noise (>105 dB)
  • Never insert plugs without washing hands first
Storage
  • Earmuffs: hang or lay flat — never squash under heavy items
  • Reusable plugs: store in the supplied case, clean before reuse
  • Keep in a dry, clean pouch away from oil, grease, and solvents
  • Carry a spare pair of disposable plugs — they get lost or damaged
  • Replace reusable plugs every 30 days minimum
PPE Category 04

Gloves

Hand injuries are among the most common on mining sites. Gloves must match the hazard: grip gloves for rod handling, leather for hot work, chemical-resistant for fuel and fluids, cut-resistant for sharps. The wrong glove type offers no real protection.

Inspect Before Use
  1. Check for cuts, holes, or worn fingertips
  2. Check leather gloves for scorch marks or stiffness
  3. Chemical gloves: inflate briefly — any leak = replace
  4. Check stitching at seams and cuffs
  5. Contaminated gloves that cannot be decontaminated must be replaced
Correct Fit
  • Gloves must fit snugly — loose gloves catch in rotating equipment
  • Cuff should cover the wrist gap with sleeves
  • Full dexterity test: pick up small object before starting task
  • Never use gloves around rotating drill string or augers
  • Right glove for right task: refer to selection table above
Storage
  • Dry thoroughly after use before storing
  • Never store near chemicals, fuel, or direct heat
  • Chemical gloves: store inverted to allow air circulation inside
  • Keep pairs clipped or banded together
  • Check MSDS for glove compatibility with any new chemical
PPE Category 05

Safety Footwear

Steel-toe boots protect against crushed toes from falling rods, drill pipe, and heavy equipment. Anti-puncture midsoles prevent nails and sharp objects from penetrating the sole. On TEMC sites, safety footwear is non-negotiable at all times outside of vehicles and accommodation.

Inspect Before Use
  1. Check sole for excessive wear or delamination
  2. Check upper for cracks, tears, or chemical damage
  3. Check steel toe cap area for visible denting (means toe cap is compromised)
  4. Check lace eyelets and laces for breakage
  5. If boots have been crushed by a load, replace even if they look intact
Correct Fit
  • 1 cm gap between longest toe and front of boot
  • Heel must not lift when walking — lace fully from bottom
  • Lace ends tucked in or secured to prevent trip hazard
  • Metatarsal protection boots for heavy fabrication and lifting zones
  • Break in new boots during light duties before high-risk tasks
Storage
  • Clean of mud and debris after each shift
  • Dry at room temperature — not near heat sources (cracks leather)
  • Condition leather uppers monthly in desert environments
  • Store upright — not on their sides (deforms the upper)
  • Replace every 12 months or earlier if wear indicators are reached
PPE Category 06

High-Visibility Vest

In active vehicle zones, near moving plant, and in low-light conditions, you must be seen. High-vis vests with retroreflective strips make you visible up to 200 metres in daylight and over 100 metres in darkness when illuminated. Class 2 minimum on all TEMC sites.

Inspect Before Use
  1. Check fluorescent fabric for fading or discolouration
  2. Check retroreflective strips for peeling, cracking, or contamination
  3. Dirty reflective strips lose up to 70% effectiveness — clean or replace
  4. Check fastening (velcro, snaps, zip) is fully functional
  5. Replace vests that are torn, faded, or have damaged reflective tape
Correct Fit
  • Vest must be fully fastened — an open vest flaps in wind and catches
  • Must fit over FR coveralls without stretching seams
  • Size up if wearing heavy layers underneath
  • No loose trailing material near rotating equipment
  • Wear over outer garment — never hidden under jacket
Storage
  • Hang when not in use — folding damages reflective strips
  • Wash per manufacturer label — industrial washing degrades fluorescence
  • Do not dry on direct heat or in full desert sun for extended periods
  • Inspect reflective strips after every 25 washes
  • Replace every 2 years or when visibility standards no longer met
PPE Category 07

Respiratory Protection

Rock drilling generates respirable silica dust at concentrations that can exceed the OEL (0.05 mg/m³) within minutes. Silicosis is fatal and has no cure. Dust masks, half-face respirators, and SCBA serve very different purposes — using the wrong one provides no real protection.

Inspect Before Use
  1. Check filter/cartridge expiry date — expired filters offer zero protection
  2. Disposable masks: check for tears, moisture, or deformation
  3. Half-face respirators: check valve flaps for cracks or debris
  4. SCBA: check cylinder pressure, harness, and regulator function
  5. Never use a respirator that has been stored with a wet filter
Correct Fit
  • Perform a positive-pressure user seal check before every use
  • Full facial hair breaks the seal — clean-shave or use hood/PAPR
  • Nose clip must be moulded closely over the nose bridge
  • Straps go over the crown and behind the neck — not parallel
  • Half-face: no space between cheekbone and edge of facepiece
Storage
  • Store in sealed plastic bag away from dust and fumes
  • Never hang on the rig where mist or dust can contaminate it
  • Half-face: remove cartridges and seal in bag when not in daily use
  • SCBA: store in dedicated cabinet, cylinder horizontal, regulator covered
  • Keep spare filters in original sealed packaging at all times
PPE Category 08

Fall Protection Harness

Required for any work above 1.8 metres including mast maintenance, scaffolding, elevated platforms, and rig substructure work. A fall arrest harness must be connected to an anchor point rated at minimum 15 kN. Harness use requires training — an incorrectly fitted harness can cause fatal suspension trauma.

Inspect Before Use
  1. Check all webbing for cuts, abrasions, heat damage, or chemical exposure
  2. Check all metal hardware — buckles, D-rings, snap hooks — for corrosion or bending
  3. Check snap hook gates — must lock positively and spring back fully
  4. Energy absorber: check indicator window — replace if deployed (orange or red zone)
  5. Lanyard: check for kinking, fraying, or chemical discolouration
  6. Any harness that has been in a fall arrest must be removed from service immediately
Correct Fit
  • Dorsal D-ring sits between shoulder blades — not at waist or neck
  • Chest strap at mid-chest level (not stomach, not throat)
  • Leg loops: two fingers should slide under but no more
  • All slack webbing must be threaded back through keepers
  • Harness must fit over your work clothing — account for FR coveralls
Storage
  • Hang on dedicated hook — never coil or bunch webbing
  • Store away from chemicals, UV, and extreme heat
  • Tag with owner name, date of issue, and date of last inspection
  • Annual formal inspection by competent person — log in register
  • Maximum service life 10 years from manufacture date (check tag)

If your PPE is damaged — here is what to do.

Never continue working with damaged or suspect PPE. A cracked hard hat provides no protection. A split glove is not a glove. Follow these four steps without exception.

1

Stop Work

Stop the task immediately. Exercise your Stop Work Authority. Continuing with damaged PPE is grounds for a safety stop.

2

Tag & Remove

Mark the damaged item with a red "DEFECTIVE — DO NOT USE" tag. Remove it from service at once. Place in the defective bin.

3

Report

Tell your supervisor and the HSE Officer immediately. Log it on the PPE Defect Report (TE-IMS-FRM-HSE-025). No reporting = no replacement budget.

4

Replace & Resume

Collect a replacement from the site PPE store. Sign the register. Confirm the replacement is serviceable before resuming the task.

Important: Any harness that has been involved in a fall arrest must be removed from service and quarantined permanently, even if no visible damage is present. The energy absorber has likely activated. Do not return it to the store.

The Golden Rules

Five rules.
Non-negotiable.

These are not suggestions. Violation of any of these rules is grounds for immediate removal from site, without exception, regardless of seniority or urgency.

1

Wear it. Every time. No exceptions.

PPE is not optional when you think it might be fine. It is mandatory for every exposure, every shift. One moment without it is the one moment something happens.

2

Inspect before you put it on.

30 seconds of checking saves a lifetime of consequences. If it is cracked, torn, expired, or deformed, it does not go on your body. Tag it and get a replacement.

3

Right PPE for the right task.

A dust mask is not a respirator for welding fumes. Safety glasses are not chemical splash goggles. Using the wrong type is as dangerous as wearing nothing. Check the selection table first.

4

Look out for your colleagues.

If you see someone without their PPE or with damaged gear, speak up. This is not dobbing in — this is looking after your team. Stop Work Authority belongs to everyone.

5

Report damage. Never improvise.

Tape over a crack, stuffing cotton in your ears, or using the wrong-size harness — these are false protections that create overconfidence. Report and replace. Never work around damaged PPE.
